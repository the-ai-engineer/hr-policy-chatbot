## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of its employees, clients, suppliers, and other individuals in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR).

1.2 This policy sets out the Company's obligations and the standards expected of all employees when handling personal data.

1.3 This policy applies to all employees, workers, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and any third parties who process personal data on behalf of the Company.

1.4 Any breach of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal in accordance with the Company's Disciplinary Policy (HR-DP-006).

## 4. Data Protection Principles

4.1 The Company will comply with the seven key principles of the UK GDPR. All personal data must be:

1. **Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently** - Individuals must be informed about how their data is used
2. **Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes** - Data must not be used for purposes incompatible with the original purpose
3. **Adequate, relevant, and limited** to what is necessary (data minimisation)
4. **Accurate and kept up to date** - Inaccurate data must be corrected or deleted without delay
5. **Kept for no longer than necessary** - See Data Retention Schedule (Section 11)
6. **Processed securely** - Appropriate technical and organisational measures must be in place
7. **Subject to accountability** - The Company must demonstrate compliance

## 5. Lawful Bases for Processing

5.1 The Company will only process personal data where it has a valid lawful basis. The six lawful bases under Article 6 of the UK GDPR are:

| Lawful Basis | Common Uses at Meridian |
|---|---|
| **Consent** | Marketing communications, optional surveys, photographs for social media |
| **Contract** | Employment contracts, payroll, benefits administration |
| **Legal obligation** | Tax reporting (HMRC), right-to-work checks, health and safety reporting |
| **Vital interests** | Emergency contact information, medical emergencies |
| **Public interest** | Not commonly used |
| **Legitimate interests** | IT security monitoring, CCTV for building security, business analytics |

5.2 Where the Company relies on consent, individuals will be informed of their right to withdraw consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

5.3 For special category data (e.g., health information, trade union membership, ethnic origin), the Company will identify both a lawful basis under Article 6 and a separate condition under Article 9 of the UK GDPR.

## 7. Subject Access Requests (SARs)

7.1 Under Article 15 of the UK GDPR, individuals have the right to obtain confirmation that their data is being processed and to access a copy of their personal data.

7.2 Subject Access Requests should be submitted in writing to the DPO at dpo@meridiantech.co.uk. Requests may also be made verbally, but written confirmation is recommended.

7.3 Employees do not need to give a reason for making a request, and there is no requirement to use a specific form. However, a SAR Request Form is available on the Company intranet to help structure requests.

7.4 The Company will respond to SARs **within 1 calendar month** of receipt. Where requests are complex or numerous, this period may be extended by a further **2 months**, but the individual will be informed of the extension and the reasons for it within the initial 1-month period.

7.5 SARs will be processed free of charge. However, the Company may charge a **reasonable fee of up to £10** for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests, or where additional copies are requested.

7.6 The Company will verify the identity of the individual before releasing any personal data. Acceptable forms of identification include a valid passport, driving licence, or two utility bills dated within the last 3 months.

7.7 The Company will not disclose personal data relating to other identifiable individuals without their consent, unless it is reasonable to do so without consent.

## 8. Data Breach Procedure

### 8.1 Definition

8.1.1 A personal data breach is a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data. Examples include:

- Sending an email containing personal data to the wrong recipient
- Loss or theft of a laptop, phone, or USB drive containing personal data
- Unauthorised access to a database or system containing personal data
- Ransomware or other cyber attack affecting personal data
- Paper records left in an unsecured location
- Verbal disclosure of personal data to an unauthorised person

### 8.2 Internal Reporting

8.2.1 All employees must report any actual or suspected data breach to the Data Protection Officer **within 24 hours** of becoming aware of it.

8.2.2 Reports should be made by email to databreach@meridiantech.co.uk or by phone to the DPO on 0117 496 2250. Outside of office hours, breaches should be reported to the IT Security team on 0117 496 2299 (24-hour helpline).

8.2.3 The report should include, as far as known:
- Description of the breach
- When and how it was discovered
- Categories and approximate number of individuals affected
- Categories and approximate number of data records affected
- Likely consequences of the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

### 8.3 ICO Notification

8.3.1 Where a data breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, the Company will notify the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) **within 72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach, as required by Article 33 of the UK GDPR.

8.3.2 The DPO will assess each breach and make the determination on whether ICO notification is required, in consultation with the IT Security team and legal counsel.

8.3.3 Notification will be made via the ICO's online breach reporting tool at https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/report-a-breach/.

### 8.4 Notification to Individuals

8.4.1 Where a breach is likely to result in a **high risk** to the rights and freedoms of individuals, the Company will notify the affected individuals without undue delay, as required by Article 34 of the UK GDPR.

8.4.2 The notification will describe the nature of the breach, the likely consequences, and the measures taken to address it, in clear and plain language.

### 8.5 Breach Register

8.5.1 The DPO will maintain a Breach Register recording all personal data breaches, regardless of whether they are reported to the ICO. The register will include the facts of the breach, its effects, and the remedial action taken.

## 9. Individual Rights

9.1 Under the UK GDPR, individuals have the following rights in relation to their personal data:

### 9.1 Right of Access
See Section 7 (Subject Access Requests).

### 9.2 Right to Rectification
9.2.1 Individuals have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal data. Employees should update their personal details via the HR self-service portal or by contacting HR directly. The Company will respond to rectification requests within **1 calendar month**.

### 9.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten")
9.3.1 Individuals have the right to request deletion of their personal data in certain circumstances, including where the data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, or where consent has been withdrawn.

9.3.2 This right does not apply where the Company is required to retain the data for legal or regulatory reasons (e.g., payroll records for HMRC purposes).

9.3.3 Requests for erasure should be submitted to the DPO. The Company will respond within **1 calendar month**.

### 9.4 Right to Restrict Processing
9.4.1 Individuals may request restriction of processing in certain circumstances, such as where accuracy is contested or processing is unlawful.

### 9.5 Right to Data Portability
9.5.1 Where processing is based on consent or contract, and is carried out by automated means, individuals have the right to receive their personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (e.g., CSV, JSON).

9.5.2 Portability requests should be submitted to the DPO and will be fulfilled within **1 calendar month**.

### 9.6 Right to Object
9.6.1 Individuals have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. The Company will stop processing unless it can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.

## 10. Employee Responsibilities

10.1 All employees who handle personal data are responsible for ensuring it is processed in accordance with this policy. Specific obligations include:

### 10.1 Physical Security
- **Clean desk:** Do not leave documents containing personal data on desks overnight or when away from your desk for extended periods. Use the secure document storage provided.
- **Printing:** Collect print jobs immediately. Use the "secure print" function for documents containing personal data.
- **Disposal:** Shred all paper documents containing personal data using the cross-cut shredders located on each floor. Never dispose of such documents in general waste bins.

### 10.2 Digital Security
- **Lock screens:** Lock your computer (Ctrl+L on Windows, Cmd+Ctrl+Q on Mac) whenever you leave your desk, even briefly.
- **Passwords:** Do not share your password with anyone, including IT support. Use unique passwords of at least 14 characters. The Company provides 1Password for all employees. See the IT Security Policy (IT-SEC-001) for full password requirements.
- **Two-factor authentication (2FA):** Must be enabled on all Company systems. This is a mandatory requirement with no exceptions.
- **Email:** Take care when addressing emails containing personal data. Use BCC when emailing multiple external recipients. Double-check the recipient before sending.
- **Removable media:** Do not store personal data on USB drives or external hard drives unless encrypted and approved by IT Security.
- **Personal devices:** Do not store Company personal data on personal devices unless enrolled in the Company's Mobile Device Management (MDM) solution.

### 10.3 Sharing Data
- Only share personal data with colleagues who have a legitimate need to access it.
- Use secure file sharing (Company SharePoint or approved cloud services) rather than email attachments where possible.
- When sharing data externally, ensure a Data Processing Agreement is in place. Contact the DPO for guidance.
- Never share personal data via instant messaging platforms not approved by the Company.

## 11. Data Retention Schedule

11.1 The following retention periods apply to the main categories of data held by the Company:

| Data Category | Retention Period | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Employee personnel files | **6 years** after termination of employment | Limitation Act 1980 |
| Payroll and tax records | **6 years** after the end of the financial year to which they relate | HMRC requirements |
| Recruitment records (unsuccessful candidates) | **6 months** from notification of outcome | ICO guidance |
| Recruitment records (successful candidates) | Transferred to personnel file; retained as above | Contractual |
| Sickness and absence records | **6 years** after termination of employment | Limitation Act 1980 |
| Maternity/paternity/adoption records | **6 years** after the relevant tax year | HMRC requirements |
| Health and safety records | **40 years** from date of incident (for RIDDOR-reportable incidents) | RIDDOR Regulations |
| Training records | **6 years** after termination of employment | Best practice |
| DBS checks | **6 months** from receipt (certificate destroyed; outcome recorded) | DBS Code of Practice |
| CCTV footage | **30 days** unless required for investigation | Legitimate interests |
| Disciplinary records | **12 months** after conclusion (warnings); **6 years** (dismissal) | Company policy / Limitation Act |
| Grievance records | **12 months** after resolution (see Grievance Policy HR-GP-007) | Company policy |
| Pension records | **6 years** after termination, or 75th birthday, whichever is later | Pensions Act |
| Interview notes | **6 months** from decision | ICO guidance |
| Equal opportunities monitoring | Anonymised and retained indefinitely for statistical purposes | Legitimate interests |

11.2 At the end of the relevant retention period, data will be securely destroyed. Paper records will be cross-cut shredded. Electronic records will be permanently deleted from all systems, including backups, within **3 months** of the retention period expiring.

## 12. CCTV

12.1 The Company operates CCTV cameras in common areas of its premises, including building entrances, reception areas, car parks, corridors, and server rooms. CCTV is **not** operated in offices, meeting rooms, kitchens, or toilet facilities.

12.2 CCTV is operated for the purposes of:
- Security of the premises and prevention of crime
- Health and safety monitoring
- Investigating incidents and complaints

12.3 CCTV signage is displayed at all entry points to areas covered by cameras.

12.4 CCTV footage is retained for **30 days** and then automatically overwritten, unless it is required for an ongoing investigation, in which case it will be retained for the duration of the investigation and any subsequent proceedings.

12.5 Requests to view CCTV footage should be directed to the DPO or the Facilities Manager.

## 13. Email and Internet Monitoring

13.1 The Company reserves the right to monitor employee use of Company email, internet, and IT systems in accordance with the Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act 2000 (RIPA) and the Telecommunications (Lawful Business Practice) (Interception of Communications) Regulations 2000.

13.2 Monitoring may be carried out for the following purposes:
- Ensuring compliance with Company policies
- Detecting unauthorised use or criminal activity
- Ensuring the effective operation of IT systems
- Investigating allegations of misconduct
- Quality assurance

13.3 The Company does **not** routinely read the content of employee emails or monitor individual browsing history. Monitoring of content will only be carried out where there is a specific, justified reason, authorised by the DPO and a member of the Senior Leadership Team.

13.4 Automated monitoring tools are used to:
- Scan emails for malware and phishing
- Block access to inappropriate or malicious websites
- Flag potential data loss (e.g., large volumes of data being sent externally)
- Monitor system performance

13.5 Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using Company IT systems.
