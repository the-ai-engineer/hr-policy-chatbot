**Policy Owner:** Finance Department

**Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
**Review Date:** 1 January 2026
**Version:** 3.2
**Classification:** Internal

## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 This policy sets out the rules and procedures for claiming reimbursement of business expenses incurred by employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") while carrying out their duties.

1.2 This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary workers engaged by the Company. Agency workers should refer to their employing agency's expenses policy unless otherwise agreed in writing.

1.3 The Company will reimburse reasonable business expenses that are wholly, exclusively, and necessarily incurred in the performance of an employee's duties, subject to the limits and conditions set out in this policy.

1.4 This policy should be read in conjunction with the Company's **Code of Conduct** (particularly Section 4 regarding Gifts and Hospitality) and the **Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy**.

---

## 2. General Principles

2.1 Employees must exercise good judgement and spend the Company's money as carefully as they would their own.

2.2 All expense claims must be supported by valid receipts or invoices. Receipts are required for all individual items exceeding **£5.00**. For items of £5.00 or under where a receipt is not available, employees must provide a written description of the expense, the date, and the business reason.

2.3 Expense claims must be submitted via the Company's expense management system (currently SAP Concur) within **30 calendar days** of the expense being incurred. Claims submitted after 60 days will not be reimbursed except in exceptional circumstances approved by a Director.

2.4 Deliberately submitting false or inflated expense claims constitutes fraud and will be treated as gross misconduct under the Company's **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure**. This may result in summary dismissal and referral to the police.

2.5 VAT receipts should be obtained wherever possible to enable the Company to reclaim VAT.

---

## 3. Approval Process

3.1 All expense claims must be approved before reimbursement as follows:

| Claim Value | Approver |
|---|---|
| Up to £500 | Line Manager |
| £500.01 - £2,000 | Department Director |
| £2,000.01 - £5,000 | Chief Financial Officer |
| Over £5,000 | Chief Executive Officer |

3.2 No employee may approve their own expenses. Where a line manager's expenses require approval, these must be submitted to their own line manager or the relevant Director.

3.3 Approved claims will be reimbursed via payroll within **14 working days** of approval, paid directly into the employee's nominated bank account.

3.4 The Finance Department reserves the right to query any expense claim and request additional information or justification before processing payment.

## 4. Travel - UK

### 4.1 General Travel Principles

4.1.1 Employees should use the most cost-effective mode of transport appropriate to the journey, taking into account the total cost (including time, productivity, and any overnight stays that might be avoided).

4.1.2 Employees should book travel as far in advance as reasonably practicable to secure the best fares. Bookings made less than 48 hours before travel may be queried by the Finance Department.

### 4.2 Rail Travel

4.2.1 Rail travel must be booked in **standard class** unless:

- The journey duration exceeds **2 hours** in one direction, in which case first class may be booked with prior line manager approval
- The employee needs to work on confidential material during the journey and no private standard-class seating is available
- The cost of a first-class advance ticket is equal to or less than the cost of a standard-class flexible ticket for the same journey

4.2.2 Employees should use the Company's corporate account with Trainline Business where available. Railcards (including the Company's corporate railcard) should be used where they offer savings.

4.2.3 Season tickets may be provided via the Company's interest-free season ticket loan scheme. Applications should be made through the HR portal. The maximum loan amount is **£5,000** per annum.

### 4.3 Mileage - Use of Personal Vehicles

4.3.1 Employees who use their own vehicle for business travel will be reimbursed at the following HMRC-approved mileage rates:

| Vehicle Type | First 10,000 miles (per tax year) | Over 10,000 miles |
|---|---|---|
| Car or van | 45p per mile | 25p per mile |
| Motorcycle | 24p per mile | 24p per mile |
| Bicycle | 20p per mile | 20p per mile |

4.3.2 Mileage claims must include the date, start location, destination, purpose of the journey, and total miles driven. The Company uses postcode-to-postcode calculation via Google Maps as the standard method for verifying distances.

4.3.3 Commuting between home and the employee's normal place of work is **not** a business expense and cannot be claimed. Where an employee travels from home to a temporary workplace, the normal commute distance must be deducted.

4.3.4 Employees using their own vehicle for business purposes must hold a valid driving licence, have appropriate business-use motor insurance, and ensure the vehicle has a valid MOT (where applicable). Proof may be requested by the Company at any time.

4.3.5 Parking charges and congestion charges incurred during business travel will be reimbursed with receipts. Parking fines and other penalties will **not** be reimbursed under any circumstances.

### 4.4 Taxis and Private Hire Vehicles

4.4.1 Taxis may be used where public transport is impractical, unavailable, or where the employee is carrying heavy equipment. Taxis may also be used for journeys starting or ending after **9:00 PM** for safety reasons.

4.4.2 Employees should use the Company's corporate Uber for Business account where available. Receipts are required for all taxi journeys.

### 4.5 Domestic Flights

4.5.1 Domestic flights within the UK may be booked in economy class where the journey by rail would exceed **4 hours** or where the total cost (including time and accommodation) is lower than rail.

4.5.2 All domestic flight bookings must be made through the Company's travel management company (currently Corporate Traveller) or with prior approval from the Finance Department.

## 5. Travel - International

### 5.1 Pre-Approval

5.1.1 All international business travel must be **pre-approved in writing** by the relevant Department Director using the International Travel Request Form (available on the HR portal) at least **10 working days** before the intended departure date.

5.1.2 The International Travel Request Form must include: destination, dates, business purpose, estimated total cost, and confirmation that any required visas or travel documents have been arranged.

### 5.2 Flights

5.2.1 International flights must be booked in **economy class** for all journeys. Premium economy may be booked with Director approval for flights exceeding **6 hours** in duration.

5.2.2 Business class is only permitted with **CEO approval** and is generally reserved for:

- Flights exceeding 8 hours where the employee must attend meetings or deliver presentations within 4 hours of landing
- Medical reasons supported by an Occupational Health recommendation

5.2.3 All international flights must be booked through the Company's travel management company.

### 5.3 International Per-Diem Rates

5.3.1 For international travel, the Company applies the following daily per-diem rates to cover meals and incidental expenses. These are paid as a flat daily allowance and individual meal receipts are not required.

| Region | Daily Per-Diem |
|---|---|
| Western Europe | £60 |
| Eastern Europe | £45 |
| North America | £75 |
| Asia-Pacific | £55 |
| Middle East and Africa | £55 |
| South America | £50 |

5.3.2 Per-diem rates are reduced by 50% for travel days (departure and return days). Where meals are provided (e.g., included in a conference registration or hotel booking), the per-diem must be reduced accordingly.

5.3.3 Where international travel extends beyond **5 consecutive days**, the employee's Director must provide additional written approval.

### 5.4 Visas and Vaccinations

5.4.1 The Company will reimburse the cost of any visa, travel vaccination, or required health documentation needed for business travel. These should be arranged through the Company's travel management company where possible.

## 6. Accommodation

6.1 Overnight accommodation may be claimed when an employee is required to work at a location that is more than **90 minutes' travel** from their home or normal place of work, and an early start or late finish makes same-day travel impractical.

6.2 Accommodation must be booked through the Company's travel management company or a pre-approved booking platform. The maximum nightly room rates (room only, including VAT) are:

| Location | Maximum Nightly Rate |
|---|---|
| Central London (within M25) | £150 |
| Other UK locations | £100 |
| International | As approved on International Travel Request Form |

6.3 Where the cost of accommodation in a particular location consistently exceeds the above limits (e.g., during peak conference periods), employees may request an uplift from the Finance Department with supporting evidence of market rates.

6.4 Employees should book accommodation that is reasonably convenient for the business venue, taking into account secondary transport costs. Staying with friends or family instead of booking a hotel does not qualify for an alternative payment.

---

## 7. Meals and Subsistence

7.1 When employees are required to work away from their normal place of work and incur additional meal costs, the following maximum reimbursement rates apply:

| Meal | Maximum Claim |
|---|---|
| Breakfast | £10.00 |
| Lunch | £15.00 |
| Dinner | £25.00 |

7.2 Meal allowances may only be claimed where the employee is working away from their normal place of work and incurs costs **additional** to their normal expenditure. Buying lunch near the office does not qualify.

7.3 Meal claims must be supported by itemised receipts. Credit card slips alone are not sufficient.

7.4 Alcoholic beverages are **not** reimbursable as part of a meal claim. The only exception is client entertainment (see Section 9).

7.5 Where meals are provided at a conference, training event, or by a client, no additional meal claim may be submitted for that meal.

---

## 8. Company Credit Cards

8.1 Company credit cards may be issued to employees at Grade 7 (Senior Manager) and above, or to other employees who regularly incur business expenses exceeding **£200 per month**, subject to Director approval.

8.2 Application for a company credit card must be made via the Finance Department using the Corporate Credit Card Application Form. All applications require approval from the employee's Director and the Finance Department.

8.3 Company credit cards must **only** be used for legitimate business expenses in accordance with this policy. Personal expenditure on a company credit card is strictly prohibited and will be treated as a disciplinary matter.

8.4 Cardholders must submit a fully reconciled statement with supporting receipts for all transactions via SAP Concur by the **5th working day** of each month for the preceding month's expenditure.

8.5 Lost or stolen cards must be reported immediately to the card issuer (currently Barclays) on **0800 XXX XXXX** and to the Finance Department. Failure to report promptly may result in the cardholder being held personally liable for fraudulent transactions.

8.6 Company credit cards must not be used for cash withdrawals except in genuine emergencies when travelling internationally, and only with prior Finance Department approval.

8.7 The Company reserves the right to withdraw a credit card at any time. Credit cards must be returned upon termination of employment or when requested by the Finance Department.

---

## 9. Client Entertainment and Hospitality

9.1 Reasonable expenditure on entertaining clients, prospective clients, or business contacts may be claimed where there is a clear business purpose.

9.2 The maximum entertainment expenditure is **£50 per person** for a meal or event. Where entertainment is expected to exceed **£200 total** (for all attendees), prior written approval from a Director is required.

9.3 Entertainment claims must include:

- Names and organisations of all attendees
- The business purpose and expected outcome
- Itemised receipt

9.4 Employees must be mindful of the Company's obligations under the **Bribery Act 2010**. Entertainment must be proportionate and must never be offered with the intention of influencing a business decision. Refer to the Company's **Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy** and Section 4 of the **Code of Conduct** for further guidance.

9.5 The Company does not reimburse entertainment of fellow employees except where:

- It is a recognised team event approved and budgeted by a Director
- A client or external party is also present

## 10. Non-Reimbursable Items

10.1 The following items will **not** be reimbursed under any circumstances:

- Personal travel, meals, or accommodation not related to Company business
- Commuting costs between home and the employee's normal place of work
- Fines, penalties, or parking tickets (including speeding fines, PCNs, and court fines)
- Alcoholic beverages (except as part of approved client entertainment under Section 9)
- Personal telephone calls (unless an emergency while travelling)
- Mini-bar charges at hotels
- In-room films or pay-per-view entertainment
- Gym or spa facilities at hotels
- Laundry and dry cleaning (unless the trip exceeds 5 consecutive nights)
- Travel insurance (the Company provides business travel insurance for all employees)
- Upgrades to travel or accommodation beyond the limits in this policy without prior approval
- Childcare or pet care costs incurred as a result of business travel
- Loss or theft of personal belongings during business travel
- Gifts for colleagues (these should be arranged through the employee's departmental budget)
- Magazine, newspaper, or book purchases
- Tips or gratuities exceeding 12.5% of the bill

## 11. Expenses for Home Workers

11.1 Employees who work from home in accordance with the Company's **Remote and Hybrid Working Policy** may claim the monthly home working allowance as set out in that policy. Equipment and one-off setup costs are also covered under that policy.

11.2 Ongoing consumables such as printer paper, ink cartridges, and stationery may be ordered through the Company's standard procurement process and are not personal expenses.

---

## 12. Monitoring and Compliance

12.1 The Finance Department will conduct regular audits of expense claims. Random audits of approximately **10%** of all claims will be carried out each quarter.

12.2 Patterns of expenditure that appear unusual, excessive, or inconsistent will be investigated. Employees may be asked to provide additional justification for any claim.

12.3 Breaches of this policy may result in:

- Refusal of the expense claim
- Recovery of amounts already paid
- Disciplinary action under the Company's **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure**, up to and including dismissal for serious or repeated breaches

## 13. Policy Review

13.1 This policy will be reviewed annually by the Finance Department in consultation with HR. HMRC mileage rates and per-diem allowances will be updated as required.

13.2 Queries about this policy should be directed to the Finance Department at **expenses@meridiantech.co.uk** or to your HR Business Partner.
