## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 This policy sets out the Company's framework for remote and hybrid working arrangements. It outlines the expectations, entitlements, and responsibilities of employees who work from home or other locations outside the Company's offices.

1.2 This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company"). It does not apply to contractors or agency workers unless specifically agreed in their contract.

1.3 The Company operates a hybrid working model that combines the benefits of in-person collaboration with the flexibility of remote working. This policy should be read in conjunction with the **Expenses and Travel Policy**, the **Code of Conduct**, and the **Health and Safety Policy**.

---

## 2. Hybrid Working Pattern

### 2.1 Standard Pattern

2.1.1 The Company's standard hybrid working pattern requires employees to work from the office for a minimum of **3 days per week** and may work remotely for up to **2 days per week**.

2.1.2 The designated office days are **Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday**. These days have been selected to maximise opportunities for in-person collaboration, team meetings, and cross-functional work.

2.1.3 Remote working days are typically **Monday and Friday**, although employees may agree alternative arrangements with their line manager where business needs permit.

2.1.4 Core office hours on designated office days are **9:00 AM to 5:30 PM**. Employees are expected to be present in the office during these hours unless they have an approved reason for absence (e.g., external meeting, annual leave, medical appointment).

### 2.2 Variations to the Standard Pattern

2.2.1 Line managers may agree variations to the standard pattern to accommodate specific business needs or individual circumstances, subject to the following principles:

- A minimum of **2 days per week** must be spent in the office (except for roles designated as fully remote under Section 3.2)
- The employee must attend the office on any day when their presence is specifically required by their manager
- Variations must not adversely affect team collaboration, client service, or the employee's performance

2.2.2 Any agreed variation must be documented in writing (email is sufficient) and reviewed at least quarterly.

## 3. Eligibility

### 3.1 General Eligibility

3.1.1 Hybrid working is available to employees who have successfully completed their **probationary period** (typically 6 months). During the probationary period, employees are expected to work from the office **5 days per week** unless their line manager agrees otherwise.

3.1.2 Eligibility for hybrid working is subject to the requirements of the employee's role. Some roles may require a greater office presence due to the nature of the work (e.g., facilities management, front-of-house, laboratory-based roles).

### 3.2 Fully Remote Roles

3.2.1 A limited number of roles may be designated as **fully remote** where the nature of the work does not require regular office presence and the employee is based more than **90 minutes' travel** from the nearest Company office. Fully remote designation must be approved by the relevant Director and HR.

3.2.2 Employees in fully remote roles are expected to attend the office at least **once per month** for team days, planning sessions, or other collaborative activities. Travel expenses for these visits will be reimbursed in accordance with the **Expenses and Travel Policy**.

### 3.3 Roles Excluded from Remote Working

3.3.1 The following types of roles are generally excluded from remote working arrangements:

- Facilities and office management
- Reception and front-of-house
- Hardware testing and laboratory roles
- Roles requiring access to secure or classified systems that cannot be accessed remotely
- Roles during the first 6 months of employment (probation period)

3.3.2 The HR Department maintains a register of role designations. Employees who are unsure of their eligibility should speak to their line manager or HR Business Partner.

## 4. Home Workspace Requirements

### 4.1 Physical Workspace

4.1.1 Employees who work from home must have access to a **suitable workspace** that enables them to work effectively and safely. As a minimum, the workspace should include:

- A dedicated desk or table of adequate size for their equipment
- A chair that provides appropriate support (see Section 5 regarding DSE assessments)
- Adequate lighting
- A reasonable level of privacy for video calls and confidential work
- Sufficient ventilation and temperature control

4.1.2 Employees are not required to have a dedicated home office, but the workspace must be free from significant distractions during working hours.

### 4.2 Internet Connectivity

4.2.1 Employees must have a reliable broadband internet connection with a minimum speed of **30 Mbps download and 5 Mbps upload**. This is necessary to support video conferencing, access to cloud-based systems, and VPN connectivity.

4.2.2 The Company does not reimburse home broadband costs, as the broadband connection is considered a personal household utility used for both personal and business purposes. The home working allowance (Section 6.1) is intended to contribute towards such costs.

4.2.3 If an employee experiences persistent connectivity issues that affect their ability to work, they should discuss this with their line manager. The Company may require the employee to work from the office until the issue is resolved.

## 5. Equipment and IT

### 5.1 Company-Provided Equipment

5.1.1 The Company will provide the following equipment to all employees eligible for hybrid or remote working:

| Item | Specification |
|---|---|
| Laptop | Company-standard laptop (currently Dell Latitude or MacBook Pro, depending on role) |
| Monitor | 24-inch external monitor |
| Keyboard | Standard external keyboard |
| Mouse | Standard external mouse |
| Headset | Noise-cancelling USB headset (for video conferencing) |

5.1.2 Equipment remains the property of the Company and must be returned upon termination of employment or when requested. Employees are responsible for the reasonable care and security of Company equipment in their possession. See Section 12 of the **Code of Conduct** for general provisions on use of Company property.

5.1.3 Replacement of faulty or damaged equipment should be reported to the IT Service Desk via the IT portal. Equipment damaged through negligence may be charged to the employee.

### 5.2 Home Office Setup Allowance

5.2.1 Employees who are approved for hybrid or remote working may claim a one-off **home office setup allowance of up to £500** to purchase additional equipment or furniture for their home workspace. This may include:

- Ergonomic office chair
- Desk or standing desk converter
- Desk lamp
- Monitor riser or laptop stand
- Webcam (if not built into the laptop)
- Other items approved by the employee's line manager

5.2.2 The setup allowance must be claimed within **6 months** of the employee becoming eligible for hybrid working. Claims should be submitted via SAP Concur with receipts, using the expense category "Home Office Setup."

5.2.3 Items purchased under the setup allowance remain the property of the Company and must be returned or offered for return upon termination of employment.

5.2.4 An additional setup allowance of up to **£250** may be claimed every **3 years** to replace or upgrade home office equipment. This must be approved by the employee's line manager.

### 5.3 Software and Systems Access

5.3.1 All employees must use the Company's VPN when accessing internal systems from outside the office. The VPN client is pre-installed on all Company laptops.

5.3.2 Employees must not store Company data on personal devices or use personal devices to access Company systems unless authorised under the Company's **Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy**.

## 6. Financial Provisions

### 6.1 Home Working Allowance

6.1.1 Employees who regularly work from home (at least one day per week) are entitled to a monthly home working allowance of **£26 per month** (£312 per annum). This is in line with the HMRC tax-free allowance for home workers and is paid through payroll free of tax and National Insurance.

6.1.2 The allowance is intended to contribute towards the additional household costs of working from home, including heating, electricity, and broadband usage.

6.1.3 The allowance is paid automatically from the first full month after the employee becomes eligible for hybrid working. It ceases if the employee moves to a fully office-based role or during extended periods of absence exceeding **4 consecutive weeks**.

6.1.4 Employees who believe their additional costs significantly exceed the standard allowance may submit a claim for actual additional costs, supported by evidence (e.g., before-and-after utility bills). Such claims are subject to approval by the Finance Department and may have tax implications.

### 6.2 No Additional Commuting Reimbursement

6.2.1 Travel between home and the Company's offices on designated office days is considered normal commuting and is **not** reimbursable under the **Expenses and Travel Policy**.

6.2.2 Travel to the office on a day that is not a designated office day (e.g., for a specific meeting or event) is also considered commuting and is not reimbursable, unless the employee is designated as fully remote under Section 3.2.

## 7. Communication and Availability

7.1 Employees working remotely are expected to be **available and responsive** during core working hours of **9:00 AM to 5:30 PM**, Monday to Friday.

7.2 The Company uses **Slack** as its primary instant messaging platform. Employees must:

- Be logged into Slack during working hours with an accurate status (Available, In a Meeting, Away, etc.)
- Respond to direct messages within **30 minutes** during core hours
- Keep their Slack profile updated with their working pattern and location

7.3 Employees must have their **camera on** during video meetings unless there is a specific reason not to (e.g., bandwidth issues, personal circumstances). Video conferencing is conducted via **Google Meet** unless a client specifies an alternative platform.

7.4 Employees should update their calendar to accurately reflect their working hours, meetings, and availability. Blocked time for focused work is encouraged and should be respected by colleagues.

7.5 Line managers should ensure regular **1-to-1 meetings** (at least fortnightly) with remote or hybrid team members to maintain communication, provide feedback, and identify any issues. At least one 1-to-1 per month should be conducted in person where possible.

## 8. Health and Safety

### 8.1 General Obligations

8.1.1 The Company has a duty of care for the health and safety of employees working from home, as it does for employees working in the office. Employees also have a responsibility to take reasonable care of their own health and safety.

8.1.2 Employees must ensure their home workspace is safe and free from hazards. This includes ensuring that trailing cables are secured, the workspace is not cluttered, and fire exits are accessible.

### 8.2 Display Screen Equipment (DSE) Assessment

8.2.1 All employees who regularly work from home must complete a **DSE self-assessment** within **4 weeks** of starting hybrid or remote working, and annually thereafter. The self-assessment form is available on the HR portal.

8.2.2 If the self-assessment identifies any issues (e.g., discomfort, inadequate equipment, poor posture), the employee should discuss these with their line manager. The Company may arrange a professional DSE assessment of the home workspace at no cost to the employee.

8.2.3 Employees are entitled to an eye test every **2 years** at the Company's expense, or more frequently if recommended by an optician. Where an optician prescribes glasses specifically for DSE use, the Company will contribute up to **£75** towards the cost.

### 8.3 Accidents and Incidents

8.3.1 Employees must report any accident, injury, or near-miss that occurs while working from home to their line manager and the Health and Safety Team within **24 hours**, using the standard incident report form.

8.3.2 The Company's employers' liability insurance covers employees while they are working from home during agreed working hours.

## 9. Data Security and Confidentiality

9.1 Employees working from home must comply with the Company's **Data Protection Policy** and **Information Security Policy** at all times.

9.2 Specific requirements for home working include:

- Using the Company VPN for all access to internal systems
- Locking the laptop screen when stepping away, even briefly
- Not allowing family members or other household members to use Company equipment
- Taking phone calls and video meetings involving confidential information in a private setting where they cannot be overheard
- Storing physical documents securely and returning them to the office for secure disposal (no confidential documents in household recycling)
- Not printing confidential documents at home unless absolutely necessary and approved by the line manager

9.3 Employees must not work on Company business from **public spaces** (e.g., cafes, libraries, co-working spaces that are not pre-approved) where screens may be visible to others or conversations overheard, unless the work does not involve confidential or personal data.

9.4 Breaches of data security while working remotely may constitute gross misconduct under the **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure**. See Section 7 of the **Code of Conduct** for further provisions on confidentiality.

## 10. International Remote Working

### 10.1 Short-Term International Remote Working

10.1.1 Employees may request to work remotely from outside the United Kingdom for a maximum of **4 weeks (20 working days) per calendar year**, subject to the following conditions:

- The employee must submit an International Remote Working Request via the HR portal at least **15 working days** before the intended start date
- Approval is required from both the employee's line manager and the HR Department
- The employee must confirm that they have the legal right to work from the proposed location
- The location must have reliable internet connectivity meeting the Company's minimum requirements (Section 4.2)
- The employee must be available during UK core hours (**9:00 AM to 5:30 PM UK time**) or agree an alternative schedule with their line manager. A time zone difference of more than **3 hours** from the UK requires Director approval

10.1.2 The **4 weeks** do not need to be taken consecutively and may be split across multiple trips, but each trip must be separately approved.

10.1.3 The Company does not provide any additional allowance or cover any additional costs (flights, accommodation, etc.) associated with international remote working. Normal salary and home working allowance continue to be paid.

### 10.2 Tax and Legal Implications

10.2.1 International remote working may have tax, social security, and employment law implications for both the employee and the Company. The HR Department will assess these as part of the approval process and may decline a request on these grounds.

10.2.2 The employee is responsible for ensuring compliance with local tax obligations in the country from which they are working. The Company accepts no liability for personal tax arising from international remote working arrangements.

10.2.3 International remote working is not permitted from countries subject to UK government sanctions or where the Company's insurers do not provide coverage. The HR Department maintains a list of excluded countries.

### 10.3 Extended International Working

10.3.1 Requests to work internationally for periods exceeding 4 weeks require approval from the relevant Director, the HR Director, and the Finance Director, and will be assessed on a case-by-case basis with consideration of tax, legal, and operational implications.

## 11. Right to Request Permanent Remote Working

### 11.1 Eligibility

11.1.1 Employees who have been employed by the Company for at least **26 weeks** may submit a formal request for permanent remote working (i.e., a change to fully remote or a variation to the standard hybrid pattern) under the statutory right to request flexible working.

### 11.2 Process

11.2.1 Requests must be submitted in writing to the employee's line manager and the HR Department, using the Flexible Working Request Form (available on the HR portal). The request should include:

- The proposed working pattern
- The date the employee would like the change to take effect
- An explanation of how the employee believes the new arrangement can work without adversely affecting the business
- Any impact on colleagues that the employee has identified and how this could be managed

11.2.2 The Company will arrange a meeting with the employee to discuss the request within **14 calendar days** of receipt. The employee has the right to be accompanied by a colleague or trade union representative.

11.2.3 The Company will notify the employee of its decision within **28 calendar days** of the meeting. If the request is approved, the change will be confirmed in writing as a permanent variation to the employee's contract of employment.

11.2.4 The Company may decline a request for one or more of the following statutory reasons:

- Burden of additional costs
- Detrimental effect on ability to meet customer demand
- Inability to reorganise work among existing staff
- Inability to recruit additional staff
- Detrimental impact on quality or performance
- Insufficiency of work during the periods the employee proposes to work
- Planned structural changes

11.2.5 If the request is declined, the Company will provide written reasons. The employee has the right to appeal within **10 working days** of receiving the decision. Appeals will be heard by a senior manager who was not involved in the original decision.

## 12. Manager Responsibilities

12.1 Line managers play a critical role in making hybrid and remote working successful. Specific responsibilities include:

- Ensuring that team meetings and collaborative activities are scheduled on office days
- Maintaining regular communication with all team members regardless of their working location
- Monitoring performance based on **outcomes and deliverables**, not physical presence or hours worked
- Identifying and addressing any issues arising from remote working (e.g., isolation, poor performance, communication gaps)
- Ensuring compliance with this policy and related policies

12.2 Line managers retain the right to **require an employee to attend the office** on any working day where there is a legitimate business reason, provided reasonable notice is given (typically at least **48 hours**, or 24 hours in urgent circumstances).

12.3 Where a manager has concerns about an employee's suitability for hybrid or remote working (e.g., due to performance issues, communication problems, or repeated failure to attend the office as required), they may, in consultation with HR, require the employee to return to full-time office working for a defined period.

## 13. Trial Periods and Review

13.1 The Company reserves the right to introduce a **3-month trial period** for any new or changed remote working arrangement. At the end of the trial, the arrangement will be reviewed and confirmed, modified, or ended.

13.2 All hybrid and remote working arrangements are subject to ongoing review and may be changed with reasonable notice if the Company's business needs require it. Significant changes to the Company's hybrid working policy will be subject to consultation.
