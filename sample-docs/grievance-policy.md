# Meridian Technologies Ltd - Grievance Policy and Procedure

## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") is committed to providing a fair, transparent, and consistent process for employees to raise concerns, problems, or complaints about their employment.

1.2 This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd, regardless of length of service, grade, or working pattern. It does not apply to agency workers, consultants, or contractors, who should raise concerns through their employing agency.

1.3 This policy does not form part of any employee's contract of employment and may be amended from time to time at the Company's discretion.

1.4 This policy should be read in conjunction with the Company's Disciplinary Policy (HR-DP-006), Anti-Bullying and Harassment Policy (HR-BH-008), and Whistleblowing Policy (HR-WB-012).

---

## 2. What Is a Grievance?

2.1 A grievance is a concern, problem, or complaint that an employee raises with the Company about any aspect of their employment. Examples include but are not limited to:

- Terms and conditions of employment
- Health and safety concerns (see also Health and Safety Policy HR-HS-003)
- Work relations and working practices
- Organisational change
- Discrimination, bullying, or harassment (see Section 10)
- New working practices or conditions
- Working environment

2.2 This policy does not cover complaints about disciplinary action taken against you. If you wish to appeal a disciplinary decision, you should use the appeal process set out in the Disciplinary Policy (HR-DP-006).

---

## 3. General Principles

3.1 The Company will treat all grievances seriously and handle them promptly, consistently, and confidentially.

3.2 No employee will be penalised or subjected to any detriment for raising a genuine grievance in good faith.

3.3 Employees have the right to be accompanied at any formal grievance hearing or appeal hearing by a trade union representative or a fellow employee of their choice (see Section 8).

3.4 Where possible, the Company will attempt to resolve grievances informally before formal procedures are invoked.

3.5 All parties involved in the grievance process are expected to act in good faith and to maintain confidentiality. Breaches of confidentiality may be treated as a disciplinary matter.

3.6 Time limits set out in this policy may be extended by mutual agreement where reasonably necessary. HR will notify the employee of any extension and the reasons for it.

---

## 4. Informal Resolution

4.1 Many concerns can be resolved quickly and informally through open communication. Before raising a formal grievance, employees are encouraged to try to resolve the matter informally.

4.2 **Step 1 - Speak to Your Line Manager:** In the first instance, employees should raise the matter with their immediate line manager. If the grievance concerns the line manager directly, the employee should approach the next level of management or a member of the HR team.

4.3 **Step 2 - HR Involvement:** If a conversation with the line manager does not resolve the issue, or if the employee is uncomfortable approaching their manager, they may contact any member of the HR team directly. The HR team can be reached at hr@meridiantech.co.uk or by visiting the HR office on the 2nd floor of the Bristol headquarters.

4.4 **Step 3 - Mediation:** At any stage, either party may request mediation as an alternative to the formal process (see Section 9).

4.5 The line manager or HR representative should aim to address the informal grievance within **5 working days** of it being raised. A brief written note of the discussion and any agreed actions will be made and shared with the employee.

---

## 5. Formal Grievance Procedure

### 5.1 Stage 1 - Submitting a Formal Grievance

5.1.1 If informal resolution has not been successful, or if the matter is too serious for informal handling, the employee may raise a formal grievance.

5.1.2 The formal grievance must be submitted **in writing** to the HR Department using the Grievance Submission Form (available on the Company intranet under HR Forms or by request from the HR team). The submission should include:

- A clear description of the grievance
- Relevant dates, times, and locations
- Names of any individuals involved
- Any steps already taken to resolve the matter informally
- The outcome the employee is seeking
- Any supporting evidence or documentation

5.1.3 The written grievance should be sent to hr@meridiantech.co.uk or delivered in a sealed envelope marked "Private and Confidential" to the HR Department.

5.1.4 Upon receipt, HR will acknowledge the grievance in writing within **2 working days** and assign a Grievance Manager to investigate the matter. The Grievance Manager will normally be a manager at least one level above the employee's line manager who has had no prior involvement in the matter.

### 5.2 Stage 2 - Investigation

5.2.1 The Grievance Manager will conduct a thorough and impartial investigation into the matters raised. The investigation will commence within **5 working days** of the grievance being received.

5.2.2 The investigation may include:

- Reviewing relevant documents, emails, or records
- Interviewing the employee who raised the grievance
- Interviewing witnesses or other relevant individuals
- Reviewing CCTV footage where applicable (in line with the Data Protection and Privacy Policy HR-DP-010)
- Consulting with HR, legal advisors, or other specialists as appropriate

5.2.3 All employees are expected to cooperate with the investigation process. Failure to do so may be treated as a disciplinary matter.

5.2.4 The investigation will be conducted as quickly as reasonably practicable. In complex cases requiring extended investigation, the employee will be kept informed of progress at least every **5 working days**.

### 5.3 Stage 3 - Grievance Hearing

5.3.1 A formal grievance hearing will be arranged within **10 working days** of the grievance being received. The employee will be given at least **3 working days' written notice** of the hearing, including:

- The date, time, and location of the hearing
- A summary of the issues to be discussed
- Their right to be accompanied (see Section 8)
- Copies of any documents that will be referred to at the hearing

5.3.2 The hearing will be chaired by the Grievance Manager, with a member of HR present to advise on procedure and take notes.

5.3.3 At the hearing, the employee will have the opportunity to:

- Explain their grievance in full
- Present any supporting evidence
- Call witnesses (with prior agreement of the Grievance Manager)
- Suggest possible resolutions

5.3.4 The hearing may be adjourned to allow for further investigation if new information comes to light.

### 5.4 Stage 4 - Outcome

5.4.1 The Grievance Manager will communicate the outcome of the grievance to the employee **in writing within 5 working days** of the hearing. The outcome letter will include:

- A summary of the grievance
- The findings of the investigation
- The decision and the reasons for it
- Any actions the Company will take
- The employee's right of appeal and the deadline for submitting an appeal

5.4.2 Possible outcomes include:

- The grievance is upheld in full, and specific remedial actions are identified
- The grievance is upheld in part, with some actions taken
- The grievance is not upheld, with reasons provided
- Further investigation is required
- Mediation is recommended

---

## 6. Appeal

6.1 If the employee is dissatisfied with the outcome of the formal grievance, they have the right to appeal.

6.2 The appeal must be submitted **in writing within 5 working days** of receiving the outcome letter. The appeal should clearly state the grounds for appeal, which may include:

- The procedure was not followed correctly
- New evidence has come to light that was not available at the time of the hearing
- The outcome was unreasonable or disproportionate
- The investigation was inadequate

6.3 The appeal will be heard by a **different senior manager** who has had no prior involvement in the grievance. In all cases, the appeal manager will be at least at Director level.

6.4 The appeal hearing will be arranged within **10 working days** of receiving the appeal. The employee will be given at least **3 working days' written notice** of the hearing.

6.5 At the appeal hearing, the employee may present their case and be accompanied in the same way as at the original hearing.

6.6 The outcome of the appeal will be communicated in writing within **5 working days** of the appeal hearing. The appeal decision is **final** and there is no further right of internal appeal.

---

## 7. Collective Grievances

7.1 Where a group of employees shares the same grievance relating to the same issue, they may submit a collective grievance.

7.2 The group should nominate a spokesperson to submit the grievance on behalf of all affected employees. The written grievance should clearly identify all individuals who are party to the collective grievance.

7.3 The procedure for collective grievances follows the same stages as individual grievances, with the following modifications:

- The spokesperson and up to **2 additional representatives** from the group may attend hearings
- The same outcome will apply to all individuals party to the collective grievance, unless individual circumstances require a different approach
- Each individual retains their own right of appeal

---

## 8. Right to Be Accompanied

8.1 At any formal grievance hearing or appeal hearing, the employee has the statutory right to be accompanied by:

- A trade union representative (whether or not the Company recognises the union), or
- A fellow employee of their choice

8.2 The companion may:

- Address the hearing to put forward the employee's case
- Sum up the employee's case
- Respond on the employee's behalf to any views expressed at the hearing
- Confer with the employee during the hearing

8.3 The companion may **not** answer questions on the employee's behalf or prevent the employee from explaining their case.

8.4 If the chosen companion is unavailable on the proposed date, the employee may request one alternative date, which must be within **5 working days** of the original date.

---

## 9. Mediation

9.1 Mediation is a voluntary, confidential process in which a neutral third party helps the parties in dispute reach a mutually acceptable resolution.

9.2 Mediation may be suggested at any stage of the grievance process by the employee, the manager, or HR. Both parties must agree to participate; mediation cannot be imposed.

9.3 The Company uses both internal trained mediators and, where appropriate, external mediators accredited by the Civil Mediation Council. The cost of external mediation will be borne by the Company.

9.4 If mediation results in a resolution, the terms will be recorded in writing and signed by both parties. The formal grievance will then be considered closed.

9.5 If mediation does not resolve the matter, the formal grievance process will continue from the point at which it was paused. Nothing said during mediation may be used as evidence in the formal process without the consent of both parties.

---

## 10. Overlap with Other Policies

### 10.1 Bullying and Harassment

10.1.1 Grievances involving allegations of bullying, harassment, or discrimination will be handled under this procedure but may also engage the Company's Anti-Bullying and Harassment Policy (HR-BH-008). HR will advise on the most appropriate process.

10.1.2 Where allegations are of a serious nature, the Company may initiate a parallel investigation under the Disciplinary Policy (HR-DP-006) against the alleged perpetrator.

### 10.2 Whistleblowing

10.2.1 If the employee's concern relates to a genuine belief that malpractice, illegality, or a danger to health and safety exists within the Company, they may wish to raise the matter under the Whistleblowing Policy (HR-WB-012) instead of or in addition to this grievance procedure.

10.2.2 The Whistleblowing Policy provides specific protections for employees who report such concerns in good faith.

### 10.3 Disciplinary Proceedings

10.3.1 Where an employee raises a grievance during ongoing disciplinary proceedings, the disciplinary process may be temporarily paused while the grievance is considered. HR will decide on a case-by-case basis whether it is appropriate to deal with both matters concurrently or sequentially.

---

## 11. Confidentiality and Record Keeping

11.1 All grievance records will be treated as confidential and handled in accordance with the Company's Data Protection and Privacy Policy (HR-DP-010) and the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR).

11.2 Grievance records, including the written grievance, investigation notes, hearing notes, outcome letters, and appeal documentation, will be retained on the employee's personal file for **12 months** after the resolution of the grievance, after which they will be securely destroyed unless there is an ongoing legal claim or regulatory requirement to retain them.

11.3 A summary record of the grievance and outcome may be retained by HR for statistical and monitoring purposes in anonymised form for up to **3 years**.

11.4 Employees have the right to request access to their grievance records in accordance with the Subject Access Request procedure set out in the Data Protection and Privacy Policy (HR-DP-010).

---

## 12. Monitoring and Review

12.1 This policy will be reviewed annually by the HR Department to ensure it remains effective and compliant with current legislation.

12.2 The Company will monitor grievance data to identify trends and take proactive steps to address any underlying workplace issues.

12.3 Anonymised grievance statistics will be reported to the Board on a quarterly basis.
