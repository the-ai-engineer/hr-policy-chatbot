# Meridian Technologies Ltd - Probation Policy

**Policy Number:** HR-PR-011
**Version:** 2.1
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
**Review Date:** 1 January 2026

## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") uses a probation period to assess whether new employees meet the required standards of performance, conduct, and attendance for their role.

1.2 The probation period provides a structured framework for new employees to receive support, clear objectives, and regular feedback during the early months of employment.

1.3 This policy applies to all new employees joining the Company from an external appointment. For internal transfers, see Section 11.

1.4 This policy does not form part of any employee's contract of employment and may be amended from time to time at the Company's discretion. However, the duration of the probation period and notice provisions are set out in the employee's contract of employment.

---

## 2. Duration of Probation

2.1 The standard probation period is **6 months** from the employee's start date.

2.2 The probation period applies to all new external hires, regardless of seniority or role, unless explicitly waived in writing by the Chief People Officer (this is exceptional and will only be considered for very senior appointments with extensive prior relationship with the Company).

2.3 For employees on fixed-term contracts of less than 12 months, the probation period will be **3 months**. For fixed-term contracts of 12 months or more, the standard 6-month probation applies.

---

## 3. Probation Objectives

3.1 The employee's line manager is responsible for setting clear, measurable probation objectives within the **first 2 weeks** of the employee's start date.

3.2 Objectives should be:
- **Specific** and clearly defined
- **Measurable** with observable outcomes
- **Achievable** within the probation period
- **Relevant** to the role and team
- **Time-bound** with milestone dates

3.3 The objectives should cover:
- Role-specific performance expectations
- Key deliverables and outputs
- Behavioural expectations, including teamwork and communication
- Completion of mandatory training (see Section 7)
- Any role-specific compliance or regulatory requirements

3.4 Objectives will be recorded on the Probation Objectives Form and a copy provided to the employee and to HR. The form is available on the Company intranet under HR > Probation > Forms.

3.5 If the employee's role or responsibilities change significantly during the probation period, objectives should be reviewed and updated accordingly.

---

## 4. Probation Reviews

4.1 Regular reviews will be held during the probation period to assess progress and provide feedback. Reviews are conducted by the employee's line manager with HR support available if needed.

### 4.1 Review Schedule

| Review | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **1-month review** | End of month 1 | Early check-in: settling in, initial impressions, any immediate concerns, confirm training plan |
| **3-month review** | End of month 3 | Mid-point assessment: progress against objectives, performance, conduct, attendance. Identify any areas of concern |
| **5-month review** | End of month 5 | Final assessment: recommendation to confirm, extend, or not confirm employment |

### 4.2 Review Process

4.2.1 Each review should be a structured meeting between the line manager and the employee. The manager should:

- Review progress against each probation objective
- Provide specific, constructive feedback on performance and conduct
- Discuss any concerns and agree any additional support or training needed
- Record the outcomes on the Probation Review Form
- Share the completed form with the employee and HR within **5 working days** of the meeting

4.2.2 Employees are encouraged to raise any concerns or requests for support during these reviews.

4.2.3 If concerns about performance, conduct, or attendance arise at any point during probation (not just at scheduled reviews), the line manager should address them promptly and contact HR for advice.

---

## 5. Notice Period During Probation

5.1 During the probation period, the notice period is **1 week** for both the employee and the Company.

5.2 This is shorter than the standard notice period that applies after successful completion of probation, which is set out in the employee's contract of employment (typically **1 month** for non-management roles and **3 months** for senior/management roles).

5.3 The Company reserves the right to make a payment in lieu of notice (PILON) during the probation period.

---

## 6. Extension of Probation

6.1 If, at the 5-month review, the line manager considers that the employee has not yet demonstrated that they fully meet the required standards but believes that improvement is achievable with additional time and support, the probation period may be **extended by up to 3 months** (i.e., to a maximum of 9 months in total).

6.2 An extension will be granted **once only**. A probation period cannot be extended beyond 9 months in total.

6.3 The decision to extend probation will be made by the line manager in consultation with HR and the Head of Department.

6.4 If probation is extended, the employee will be notified in writing, and the notification will include:

- The reasons for the extension
- The length of the extension (up to 3 months)
- Specific areas for improvement
- Updated or new objectives for the extension period
- Support that will be provided (e.g., additional training, coaching, mentoring)
- A clear statement that failure to meet the required standard by the end of the extension may result in termination of employment
- The date of the next review meeting

6.5 During the extension period, at least one further formal review meeting will be held, typically at the midpoint of the extension.

6.6 Terms and conditions during the extended probation period remain the same as during the initial probation period, including the 1-week notice period and benefit restrictions (see Section 7).

## 7. Benefits During Probation

7.1 Employees during their probation period are entitled to the following:

| Benefit | During Probation | After Probation |
|---|---|---|
| Salary | Full contractual salary | Full contractual salary |
| Annual leave | **25 days** per year (pro-rata), accrued monthly as normal | 25 days (+ option to buy/sell) |
| Pension | Auto-enrolled from day 1 (statutory minimum) | Enhanced Company scheme (6% employer contribution) |
| Sick pay | Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) only | Company Sick Pay scheme |
| Employee Assistance Programme | Full access from day 1 | Full access |
| Death in Service | Covered from day 1 (4x salary) | Covered (4x salary) |
| Mandatory training | Fully funded | Fully funded |
| Discretionary training budget | **Not available** | £2,000 per year (see L&D Policy HR-LD-009) |
| Study leave | **Not available** | Up to 5 days per year |
| Mentoring programme | **Not available** | Available |
| Certification bonuses | **Not available** | Available |

### 7.2 Restricted Benefits

7.2.1 The following benefits are **not available** during the probation period:

- **Private medical insurance:** Cover commences on the first day of the month following successful completion of probation. The employee will receive enrolment details from the HR team. The Company's scheme is with Bupa, providing cover for the employee, with the option to add dependants at the employee's expense.

- **Buying or selling annual leave:** The Company's flexible leave scheme (buying up to 5 additional days or selling up to 3 days) is not available during probation. This benefit becomes available from the start of the next full leave year following confirmation in post.

- **Discretionary training budget (£2,000):** The individual training budget under the Learning and Development Policy (HR-LD-009) is not available during probation. Mandatory training is unaffected.

- **Study leave:** Paid study leave is not available during probation (see Learning and Development Policy HR-LD-009, Section 6).

- **Enhanced Company pension contributions:** The Company's enhanced pension scheme (6% employer, 4% employee) replaces the statutory auto-enrolment scheme upon successful completion of probation. The employee will be moved to the enhanced scheme from the first day of the month following confirmation.

- **Company Sick Pay:** During probation, employees receive Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) only. The Company Sick Pay scheme (up to 4 weeks full pay and 4 weeks half pay per rolling 12-month period) applies after successful completion of probation.

- **Sabbatical eligibility:** Sabbatical leave (available after 5 years' service) does not count probation time differently, but employees cannot apply during probation.

7.2.2 For the avoidance of doubt, the following benefits **are** available from day 1:
- Annual leave (accrued as normal)
- Pension (statutory auto-enrolment)
- Employee Assistance Programme (Health Assured, 0800 028 0199)
- Death in Service cover
- Cycle to Work scheme
- Season ticket loan (after 1 month's service)

## 8. Successful Completion of Probation

8.1 Following the 5-month review (or the final review during an extension period), where the line manager is satisfied that the employee has met the required standards, the line manager will recommend confirmation of employment to HR.

8.2 HR will issue a **written confirmation letter** to the employee within **5 working days** of the recommendation, confirming:

- Successful completion of probation
- The date from which the probation period is considered complete
- The employee's notice period going forward (as per contract of employment)
- Access to full employee benefits (see Section 7)

8.3 Upon confirmation:
- The employee's notice period changes to the contractual notice period (typically 1 month for non-management, 3 months for senior/management)
- Private medical insurance enrolment is processed (cover from the 1st of the following month)
- Enhanced pension contributions commence (from the 1st of the following month)
- Company Sick Pay scheme applies
- The individual training budget (£2,000 per year) becomes available, pro-rated for the remainder of the financial year
- The employee becomes eligible for the mentoring programme, study leave, and certification bonuses
- The employee becomes eligible for the flexible leave scheme from the next full leave year

## 9. Unsuccessful Probation

9.1 If, following the review process (including any extension), the line manager concludes that the employee has not met the required standards, the Company may terminate the employee's employment.

### 9.1 Process

9.1.1 Before any decision to terminate employment is made, the following steps will be taken:

1. **Probation outcome meeting:** The employee will be invited to a meeting with their line manager and a member of the HR team. The invitation will be in writing, giving at least **3 working days' notice**, and will:
   - State the purpose of the meeting (to discuss the outcome of probation)
   - Summarise the concerns about performance, conduct, or attendance
   - Inform the employee of their **right to be accompanied** by a trade union representative or a fellow employee

2. **The meeting:** At the meeting, the line manager will:
   - Explain the specific areas where the employee has not met the required standard
   - Refer to feedback given at previous review meetings
   - Give the employee the opportunity to respond, present any mitigating circumstances, and ask questions
   - Adjourn to consider the employee's representations before making a final decision

3. **Decision:** The line manager, in consultation with HR, will decide on one of the following outcomes:
   - **Confirm employment** (if the employee's representations are sufficient)
   - **Extend probation** (if not already extended; see Section 6)
   - **Terminate employment** with the required notice (1 week) or payment in lieu of notice

4. **Written confirmation:** The outcome will be confirmed in writing within **5 working days** of the meeting. If employment is terminated, the letter will include:
   - The reasons for the decision
   - The effective date of termination
   - Details of the notice period or payment in lieu
   - Details of any outstanding pay, accrued holiday pay, and return of Company property
   - The employee's right to appeal

### 9.2 Right of Appeal

9.2.1 The employee has the right to appeal the decision to terminate their employment during probation.

9.2.2 The appeal must be submitted in writing to the Head of HR within **5 working days** of receiving the termination letter. The appeal should state the grounds for the appeal.

9.2.3 The appeal will be heard by a senior manager who has had no prior involvement in the probation process, within **10 working days** of receiving the appeal.

9.2.4 The employee has the right to be accompanied at the appeal hearing.

9.2.5 The outcome of the appeal will be communicated in writing within **5 working days** of the appeal hearing. The appeal decision is **final**.

## 10. Absence During Probation

10.1 Attendance is monitored closely during the probation period. Any absence (other than pre-agreed annual leave) may extend the probation period by an equivalent number of working days, at the Company's discretion, to ensure the employee has had a fair opportunity to demonstrate their capabilities.

10.2 If an employee is absent for more than **10 working days** during the probation period (whether continuous or aggregate), the probation period will automatically be extended by the number of days absent.

10.3 Employees should report all sickness absence in accordance with the Company's Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-004). During probation, employees are entitled to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) only (see Section 7).

---

## 11. Internal Transfers

11.1 Employees transferring to a new role within the Company are **not** subject to a new probation period if they have:

- Completed their original probation period, **and**
- **2 or more years' continuous service** with the Company at the time of transfer

11.2 Employees who have less than 2 years' continuous service at the time of an internal transfer, but have completed their original probation, will serve a **3-month adjustment period** (not a formal probation) in their new role. During the adjustment period:

- Full benefits continue to apply (no restrictions)
- Regular check-ins will be held with the new line manager
- If the move is unsuccessful, the Company will make reasonable efforts to find the employee an alternative suitable role, though this cannot be guaranteed

11.3 Employees who have not yet completed their original probation and transfer to a new role will recommence a full 6-month probation period in the new role.

## 12. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Line Manager** | Set objectives within 2 weeks; conduct reviews at 1, 3, and 5 months; provide regular feedback and support; recommend outcome to HR |
| **Employee** | Engage fully with the probation process; raise any concerns promptly; complete mandatory training; work towards objectives |
| **HR** | Provide guidance and support to managers; ensure process is followed; issue confirmation or termination letters; manage benefit enrolments |
| **Head of Department** | Approve any extension of probation; involved in decisions to terminate employment where appropriate |

---

## 13. Monitoring and Review

13.1 HR will track the following metrics:
- Number of employees in probation at any time
- Probation pass, extension, and termination rates by department
- Timeliness of objective setting and reviews
- Time to confirmation letter after recommendation

13.2 Probation data will be reported to the Senior Leadership Team quarterly.

13.3 This policy will be reviewed annually by the HR Department.
