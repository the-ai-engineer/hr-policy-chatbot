## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out the provisions for various types of leave of absence that do not fall under the Company's Annual Leave Policy, Sickness Absence Policy, or Parental Leave Policy. Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") recognises that employees may need time away from work for personal, civic, or family reasons, and aims to support them while maintaining business continuity.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd. Some provisions are dependent on length of service, as indicated in the relevant sections.

For annual leave, please refer to the **Annual Leave Policy (HR-AL-001)**. For sickness absence, please refer to the **Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-002)**. For maternity, paternity, adoption, and shared parental leave, please refer to the **Parental Leave Policy (HR-PL-003)**.

## 3. Compassionate Leave

### 3.1 Immediate Family

Employees are entitled to up to **5 days' paid compassionate leave** in the event of the death of an immediate family member. For the purposes of this policy, immediate family includes:

- Spouse, civil partner, or partner
- Child (including stepchild or adopted child)
- Parent (including step-parent or parent-in-law)
- Sibling (including step-sibling)

This entitlement is per bereavement event and may be taken flexibly within **4 weeks** of the death or funeral, with the agreement of the line manager.

### 3.2 Extended Family and Close Relationships

Employees are entitled to up to **1 day's paid compassionate leave** in the event of the death of a member of their extended family or a close personal relationship. This includes:

- Grandparent or grandchild
- Aunt, uncle, niece, or nephew
- Close friend
- Former guardian

### 3.3 Critical Illness

Where an immediate family member is critically ill (defined as a condition where there is a genuine risk to life), up to **3 days' paid compassionate leave** may be granted. Further time off may be granted as annual leave or unpaid leave at the line manager's discretion.

### 3.4 Additional Time

Where the standard compassionate leave entitlement is insufficient, the line manager may approve additional time off as:
- Annual leave
- Unpaid leave
- A combination of both

Employees are also reminded of the **Employee Assistance Programme** (see **Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-002)**, Section 10), which provides confidential counselling support.

### 3.5 Parental Bereavement

Employees who suffer the loss of a child under 18 are entitled to statutory parental bereavement leave as set out in the **Parental Leave Policy (HR-PL-003)**, Section 14. This is in addition to the compassionate leave provided under this section.

## 4. Emergency Dependants Leave

### 4.1 Entitlement

All employees are entitled to take a **reasonable amount of unpaid time off** to deal with an emergency involving a dependant. This is a statutory right available from the first day of employment.

### 4.2 Definition of a Dependant

A dependant is the employee's:
- Spouse, civil partner, or partner
- Child (of any age)
- Parent
- Any person who lives in the same household as the employee (excluding lodgers, tenants, or employees)
- Any person who reasonably relies on the employee for care

### 4.3 Qualifying Emergencies

Time off may be taken when a dependant:
- Falls ill or is injured
- Is involved in an incident during school hours
- Gives birth unexpectedly
- Dies
- Requires the employee to make urgent care arrangements due to the unexpected disruption or termination of existing care arrangements

### 4.4 Duration

The right is to a **reasonable** amount of time off. In most cases, this will be **1 to 2 days** to deal with the immediate emergency and put longer-term arrangements in place. It is not intended to provide extended time off for ongoing care.

### 4.5 Notification

Employees must inform their line manager **as soon as reasonably practicable** of the need for time off, the reason for it, and how long they expect to be absent.

### 4.6 Pay

Emergency dependants leave is **unpaid** under statute. However, the Company will exercise discretion and will normally pay for the **first day** of emergency dependants leave in any 12-month period. Any further days will be unpaid, or the employee may request to use annual leave.

## 5. Jury Service

### 5.1 Entitlement

Employees who are summoned for jury service are entitled to time off to fulfil this civic duty. The Company will grant **paid leave for the full duration** of jury service.

### 5.2 Notification

Employees must inform their line manager and the People & Culture Team **as soon as possible** upon receipt of a jury summons, and must provide a copy of the summons.

### 5.3 Pay Arrangements

The Company will continue to pay the employee's normal basic salary during jury service. In return, the employee must:

(a) Claim the **loss of earnings allowance** from the court
(b) **Repay the court's loss of earnings allowance** to the Company upon receipt

The employee should submit the court's Certificate of Loss of Earnings form to the People & Culture Team before attending court, so that the Company can confirm the employee's daily rate of pay.

### 5.4 Returning to Work

On any day where the employee is not required by the court for the full day, they are expected to return to work for the remainder of the day, where this is reasonably practicable.

### 5.5 Postponement

If jury service would cause significant disruption to the business (e.g., during a critical project phase or where multiple team members are absent), the Company may ask the employee to apply to the court for a postponement. The Company will provide a supporting letter. The final decision rests with the court.

## 6. Medical and Dental Appointments

### 6.1 General Principle

Employees should, wherever possible, arrange routine medical and dental appointments **outside of working hours** or at the beginning or end of the working day to minimise disruption.

### 6.2 Routine Appointments

Where it is not possible to arrange an appointment outside working hours, the employee should give their line manager at least **48 hours' notice**. Time taken for routine appointments will normally be treated as **paid time off**, but employees may be asked to make up the time if the appointment takes a significant portion of the working day (more than 2 hours).

### 6.3 Ongoing Treatment

Time off for hospital appointments, specialist consultations, or ongoing treatment related to a medical condition will be treated sympathetically and will normally be granted as **paid time off** without the requirement to make up the time. Evidence of the appointment may be requested.

### 6.4 Screening and Vaccinations

The Company encourages employees to attend cancer screening, health checks, and vaccination appointments. Reasonable paid time off will be granted for these purposes.

### 6.5 Recording

All time off for medical and dental appointments should be recorded on **PeopleHub**, regardless of duration.

## 7. Public Duties

### 7.1 Entitlement

Employees who hold certain public positions are entitled to reasonable time off to perform those duties. The Company will grant up to **12 days' paid leave per calendar year** for the performance of public duties. Public positions covered by this provision include:

- Justice of the Peace (Magistrate)
- Local councillor (parish, district, county, or unitary authority)
- Member of a statutory tribunal (e.g., employment tribunal)
- Governor of a maintained school or academy
- Member of a health authority or NHS trust board
- Member of the Environment Agency
- Member of a police and crime panel
- Member of a prison independent monitoring board

### 7.2 Notification

Employees must inform their line manager and the People & Culture Team of their public role and provide reasonable advance notice of dates when they will be absent. A schedule of expected commitments should be provided at the beginning of each calendar year where possible.

### 7.3 Additional Time

If the employee's public duties require more than 12 days per year, additional time off may be granted as:
- Annual leave
- Unpaid leave
- Flexible working arrangements (e.g., adjusted hours)

This will be discussed and agreed with the line manager and the People & Culture Team.

## 8. Religious Observance

### 8.1 Entitlement

The Company respects the religious beliefs and practices of all employees. Employees who wish to take time off for religious observance (e.g., holy days, festivals, or pilgrimages) that is not covered by UK public holidays may request up to **3 days' unpaid leave per calendar year** for this purpose.

### 8.2 Alternatives

Employees may alternatively use:
- Annual leave (from their standard entitlement)
- Time purchased through the buy/sell leave scheme (see **Annual Leave Policy (HR-AL-001)**, Section 9)
- Flexible working arrangements (e.g., making up the time at other times)

### 8.3 Notification

Requests should be made at least **4 weeks in advance** through PeopleHub. The Company will make every reasonable effort to accommodate requests, subject to business needs.

### 8.4 Prayer and Worship During the Working Day

Employees who wish to observe daily prayers or other religious practices during the working day should discuss this with their line manager to agree appropriate arrangements (e.g., use of a quiet room, flexible break times). Such arrangements should not significantly disrupt work or reduce the employee's total working hours.

## 9. Career Breaks

### 9.1 Eligibility

Employees with at least **3 years' continuous service** and a satisfactory performance record may apply for an unpaid career break.

### 9.2 Duration

Career breaks may be granted for a minimum of **3 months** and a maximum of **12 months**. The duration will be agreed in writing before the career break commences.

### 9.3 Reasons

Career breaks may be requested for purposes including (but not limited to):
- Extended travel
- Personal development or study
- Caring responsibilities
- Volunteering or charitable work
- Personal projects

Career breaks will **not** be granted for the purpose of taking up alternative employment elsewhere, unless agreed in exceptional circumstances.

### 9.4 Terms During the Career Break

(a) The employee's contract of employment will be **suspended** (not terminated) for the duration of the career break. Continuous service will be paused and will resume on the employee's return.

(b) The employee will **not** receive salary or accrue annual leave, sick pay, or pension contributions during the career break.

(c) The employee may **retain access** to company benefits (private medical insurance, life assurance) at their own cost, subject to the terms of the relevant schemes.

(d) The employee must **not undertake paid employment** with another employer during the career break without the prior written consent of the Company.

### 9.5 Return to Work

(a) The Company will make every reasonable effort to provide the employee with the **same or a comparable role** on their return. However, the Company cannot guarantee that the exact same role will be available, particularly after career breaks of 6 months or more.

(b) The employee must confirm their intention to return at least **8 weeks before** the agreed return date.

(c) If the employee decides not to return, they must give notice in accordance with their contract of employment.

### 9.6 Application Procedure

Applications for career breaks should be submitted in writing to the line manager and the People & Culture Team at least **3 months** before the proposed start date. Approval is at the discretion of the Head of Department and the People & Culture Director, taking into account business needs, team coverage, and the employee's performance and conduct record.

## 10. Volunteering Leave

### 10.1 Entitlement

All employees are entitled to **2 days' paid volunteering leave** per calendar year to undertake voluntary work with a registered charity, community organisation, or social enterprise.

### 10.2 Eligible Activities

Volunteering leave may be used for activities such as:
- Volunteering at a food bank, homeless shelter, or community centre
- Supporting a charity event or fundraiser
- Mentoring or teaching in a school or youth organisation
- Environmental conservation work
- Pro bono professional services for a charitable organisation

Volunteering leave may not be used for political campaigning, religious proselytising, or activities that conflict with the Company's values or Code of Conduct.

### 10.3 Procedure

Employees must submit a request through PeopleHub at least **2 weeks in advance**, specifying the organisation and the nature of the voluntary work. Approval is subject to business needs.

### 10.4 Carry-Over

Volunteering leave cannot be carried over to the following year and will be forfeited if not used.

## 11. Moving House

### 11.1 Entitlement

Employees are entitled to **1 day's paid leave** per calendar year for the purpose of moving to a new home. This applies to moves to a new primary residence only (not second homes or investment properties).

### 11.2 Notification

At least **1 week's notice** must be given, and the employee may be asked to provide evidence of the move (e.g., a completion letter from a solicitor or a copy of a new tenancy agreement).

### 11.3 Frequency

This entitlement is limited to **one occasion per calendar year**. Additional moves within the same year should be covered by annual leave.

## 12. Study Leave

### 12.1 Professional Qualifications

Where the Company has approved and is sponsoring an employee's professional qualification or course of study, paid study leave will be granted in accordance with the terms of the individual learning agreement. As a guideline:

- **Exam preparation:** Up to 2 days' paid study leave per exam, to be taken in the week immediately preceding the exam
- **Exam day:** Paid time off on the day of the exam

### 12.2 Non-Sponsored Study

Employees undertaking courses of study that are not sponsored by the Company may request annual leave or unpaid leave for exam preparation and attendance.

## 13. Reserve Forces Leave

### 13.1 Entitlement

Employees who are members of the Reserve Forces (Territorial Army, Royal Naval Reserve, Royal Marines Reserve, or Royal Auxiliary Air Force) are entitled to up to **15 days' paid leave per year** for annual training commitments.

### 13.2 Mobilisation

If the employee is mobilised for active service, their employment will be protected under the Reserve Forces (Safeguard of Employment) Act 1985. The Company will comply with its obligations under this legislation and will support the employee and their family during the period of mobilisation.

### 13.3 Notification

Employees should inform their line manager and the People & Culture Team of their Reserve Forces membership and provide a schedule of anticipated training commitments at the beginning of each year.

## 14. Time Off for Trade Union Duties

### 14.1 Entitlement

Employees who are accredited trade union representatives are entitled to reasonable paid time off to carry out trade union duties and to undergo relevant training, in accordance with Section 168 of the Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992.

### 14.2 Trade Union Activities

Trade union members (not only representatives) are entitled to reasonable **unpaid** time off for trade union activities, such as attending branch meetings or voting in union elections.

## 15. General Provisions

### 15.1 Abuse of Leave Provisions

Any employee found to have abused the provisions of this policy (e.g., providing false information to obtain leave, using leave for purposes other than those stated) may be subject to disciplinary action, which could include dismissal.

### 15.2 Record Keeping

All leave taken under this policy must be recorded on **PeopleHub**. Line managers are responsible for ensuring accurate recording.

### 15.3 Interaction with Other Policies

Where the provisions of this policy overlap with other Company policies, the more specific policy will take precedence. In cases of doubt, employees should contact the People & Culture Team.

### 15.4 Discretion

The Company reserves the right to exercise discretion in the application of this policy, taking into account individual circumstances, the needs of the business, and the employee's length of service and conduct record. Any discretionary decisions will be made fairly and consistently.
