## 1. Purpose

1.1 Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") is committed to supporting employees who are unable to work due to illness or injury. This policy sets out the Company's procedures for managing sickness absence, the support available to employees, and the steps that will be taken where absence levels give cause for concern.

1.2 The Company recognises that employees may from time to time be unable to attend work due to genuine illness. However, regular and reliable attendance is essential to the effective operation of the business, and high levels of absence can place a significant burden on colleagues and the organisation.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd, regardless of length of service, grade, or working pattern. It does not apply to contractors, consultants, or agency workers.

For provisions relating to annual leave during sickness, please refer to the **Annual Leave Policy (HR-AL-001)**. For maternity-related illness, please refer to the **Parental Leave Policy (HR-PL-003)**.

## 3. Employee Responsibilities

### 3.1 Notification Procedure

On the first day of absence, employees must:

(a) **Telephone their line manager** (or the designated alternative) **before their normal start time** or no later than **30 minutes after** their normal start time.

(b) Notification must be made by **telephone call only**. Text messages, emails, WhatsApp messages, or Slack messages are **not acceptable** as a first notification of absence, except where the employee's medical condition makes a telephone call genuinely impossible (e.g., loss of voice), in which case a text or email should be sent explaining why a call is not possible.

(c) If the line manager is unavailable, the employee should leave a voicemail and also contact the **departmental administrator** or the **People & Culture Team** on **0121 496 3200**.

(d) The employee should provide:
- The reason for the absence
- The expected duration of the absence (if known)
- Any urgent work that requires cover or handover
- A contact number if different from the one held on file

### 3.2 Ongoing Notification

3.2.1 For absences of more than one day, the employee must contact their line manager on **each subsequent working day** of absence, unless otherwise agreed with the manager.

3.2.2 For absences expected to last more than 7 calendar days, the employee and manager should agree a reasonable frequency for ongoing contact (typically at least once per week).

### 3.3 Medical Certification

(a) For absences of **7 calendar days or fewer** (including weekends and non-working days), the employee must complete a **self-certification form** via PeopleHub on their first day back at work or, if the absence exceeds 7 days, before the 8th calendar day.

(b) For absences exceeding **7 calendar days**, the employee must provide a **fit note** (Statement of Fitness for Work) from their GP or hospital doctor. The fit note must be submitted to the line manager or uploaded to PeopleHub within **3 working days** of being issued.

(c) Fit notes must cover the entire period of absence without gaps. If a fit note expires and the employee remains unfit for work, a new fit note must be obtained and submitted promptly.

(d) Where a fit note states that an employee "may be fit for work" with certain adjustments, the Company will consider whether those adjustments can reasonably be accommodated. The line manager will discuss this with the employee and the People & Culture Team.

## 4. Return-to-Work Meetings

4.1 A return-to-work meeting will be conducted by the line manager **after every period of sickness absence**, regardless of duration.

4.2 The meeting should normally take place on the **first day of the employee's return** or as soon as reasonably practicable (and no later than 2 working days after the return).

4.3 The purposes of the return-to-work meeting are to:
- Welcome the employee back to work
- Confirm the reason for the absence and update the absence record
- Ensure the employee is fit to return and identify any support or adjustments needed
- Bring the employee up to date on any work developments during their absence
- Where relevant, discuss attendance levels and any concerns under this policy

4.4 A record of the return-to-work meeting will be kept on the employee's file. The employee will be given a copy.

## 5. Sick Pay Entitlements

### 5.1 Company Sick Pay

Employees are entitled to Company Sick Pay (CSP) based on their length of continuous service as at the first day of absence, as follows:

| Continuous Service | Full Pay | Half Pay | Total Entitlement |
|---|---|---|---|
| During probation (first 6 months) | Statutory Sick Pay only | - | As per SSP rules |
| 6 months to 1 year | 1 month | 1 month | 2 months |
| 1 year to 2 years | 2 months | 2 months | 4 months |
| 2 years to 3 years | 3 months | 3 months | 6 months |
| 3 years to 5 years | 4 months | 4 months | 8 months |
| 5 years or more | 6 months | 6 months | 12 months |

5.2 Company Sick Pay entitlements are calculated on a rolling 12-month basis. Full pay means the employee's normal basic salary. Half pay means 50% of the employee's normal basic salary.

5.3 Company Sick Pay is inclusive of any Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) entitlement. It is not paid in addition to SSP.

### 5.2 Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)

5.2.1 Where an employee has exhausted their Company Sick Pay entitlement, or is not eligible for CSP, they may be entitled to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP), currently **£116.75 per week** (2025/26 rate), for up to 28 weeks in any period of incapacity for work.

5.2.2 SSP is payable from the **4th qualifying day** of absence. The first 3 qualifying days are "waiting days" for which no SSP is payable.

5.2.3 Employees who are not eligible for SSP will be issued a form SSP1 enabling them to claim Employment and Support Allowance from the Department for Work and Pensions.

### 5.3 Abuse of Sick Pay

The Company takes a serious view of any abuse of the sickness absence or sick pay provisions. Any employee found to have made a fraudulent claim for sick pay, or to have been absent when not genuinely unwell, may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including summary dismissal.

## 6. Trigger Points

6.1 The Company monitors sickness absence levels and will take action where absence reaches certain trigger points. The trigger points are designed to identify patterns of absence that may indicate an underlying problem requiring support, or attendance levels that are unsatisfactory.

6.2 The trigger points are:

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Frequency | **4 or more separate periods** of absence in any rolling 12-month period |
| Duration | **10 or more working days** of absence in any rolling 12-month period |
| Continuous absence | Any single period of absence lasting **4 or more consecutive weeks** (28 calendar days) |
| Pattern | A discernible pattern of absence (e.g., regular Mondays or Fridays, absences around bank holidays, or absences coinciding with declined annual leave requests) |

6.3 Where a trigger point is reached, the line manager will discuss the matter with the People & Culture Team and will normally invite the employee to a **Stage 1 meeting** under the Staged Absence Procedure (Section 7).

6.4 Absences related to disability, pregnancy, or work-related injury will be recorded separately and will not normally count towards the trigger points, though the overall level of absence may still be reviewed.

## 7. Staged Absence Procedure

### 7.1 General Principles

(a) The staged procedure is intended to be supportive as well as procedural. The aim is to help employees improve their attendance where possible, while ensuring that the Company can manage its operations effectively.

(b) At each stage, the employee has the right to be accompanied by a trade union representative or a workplace colleague.

(c) At each stage, reasonable adjustments will be considered for employees with a disability under the Equality Act 2010.

### 7.2 Stage 1 - First Attendance Review Meeting

7.2.1 The employee will be invited to a Stage 1 meeting in writing, giving at least **5 working days' notice**. The letter will set out the reason for the meeting, the employee's absence record, and the right to be accompanied.

7.2.2 At the meeting, the line manager will:
- Review the employee's absence record
- Discuss the reasons for the absences
- Explore whether there are any underlying health issues, work-related factors, or personal circumstances contributing to the absence
- Consider whether a referral to Occupational Health is appropriate (see Section 9)
- Consider whether any workplace adjustments or support may help
- Remind the employee of the Employee Assistance Programme (see Section 10)

7.2.3 Following the meeting, the manager may issue a **Stage 1 Attendance Improvement Note**, which will:
- Confirm the employee's absence record and the concerns discussed
- Set an attendance target over a **review period of 3 months**
- Specify the improvement expected (e.g., no more than 2 periods of absence in 3 months)
- Confirm that failure to improve may result in progression to Stage 2

7.2.4 A Stage 1 Attendance Improvement Note will remain active for **6 months** from the date of issue. If attendance is satisfactory during the review period, no further action will be taken, though the note will remain on file.

### 7.3 Stage 2 - Second Attendance Review Meeting

7.3.1 If the employee's attendance does not improve sufficiently during the Stage 1 review period, or if a trigger point is reached again within 6 months of a Stage 1 note, the employee will be invited to a **Stage 2 meeting**.

7.3.2 The same procedure as Stage 1 will be followed. The meeting will be conducted by the line manager and a member of the People & Culture Team.

7.3.3 Following the meeting, the manager may issue a **Stage 2 Attendance Improvement Note** (equivalent to a written warning), which will:
- Set a further review period of **3 months**
- Specify the attendance improvement required
- Warn the employee that failure to achieve and maintain the required level of attendance may result in progression to Stage 3, which may result in dismissal

7.3.4 A Stage 2 Attendance Improvement Note will remain active for **9 months** from the date of issue.

### 7.4 Stage 3 - Final Attendance Review Meeting

7.4.1 If the employee's attendance remains unsatisfactory after Stage 2, or if a trigger point is again reached during the Stage 2 review period, the employee will be invited to a **Stage 3 Final Attendance Review Meeting**.

7.4.2 The Stage 3 meeting will be conducted by a **Head of Department** (or above) and a senior member of the People & Culture Team.

7.4.3 At the Stage 3 meeting, all options will be considered, including:
- Further adjustments or support
- Redeployment to a different role, where available and appropriate
- An extended review period
- **Dismissal on grounds of capability** (due to ill health)

7.4.4 If dismissal is the outcome, the employee will be given their contractual or statutory notice (whichever is greater) and will be informed of their right to appeal.

### 7.5 Right of Appeal

At each stage of the procedure, the employee has the right to appeal against the outcome. Appeals must be submitted in writing to the **People & Culture Director** within **10 working days** of receiving the outcome letter. Appeals will be heard by a manager who was not involved in the original decision.

## 8. Long-Term Sickness Absence

### 8.1 Definition

For the purposes of this policy, long-term sickness absence is defined as a continuous absence of **4 weeks (28 calendar days) or more**.

### 8.2 Maintaining Contact

8.2.1 The Company will maintain reasonable and supportive contact with the employee throughout their absence. The line manager will normally make contact at least **once every two weeks**, or at such frequency as agreed with the employee.

8.2.2 Contact may be by telephone, email, letter, or (if the employee agrees) a home visit. The Company will respect the employee's wishes regarding the method and frequency of contact where reasonable.

8.2.3 The employee is expected to respond to reasonable contact from their manager and to keep the Company informed of their condition and any changes in their expected return date.

### 8.3 Occupational Health Referral

In cases of long-term absence, the Company will normally refer the employee to the Company's Occupational Health provider (see Section 9). The purpose of the referral is to obtain a medical opinion on:
- The nature of the employee's condition
- The likely duration of the absence
- Whether the employee is likely to be able to return to their current role
- Whether any reasonable adjustments could facilitate a return to work
- Whether the condition may constitute a disability under the Equality Act 2010

### 8.4 Long-Term Absence Review Meetings

The Company will hold periodic review meetings with the employee (normally every **4 to 6 weeks**) to discuss progress, the Occupational Health report, and options for supporting a return to work. These meetings may be held at the workplace, at the employee's home (with their agreement), or by telephone/video call.

### 8.5 Phased Return to Work

8.5.1 Where an employee is returning from a period of long-term sickness absence, the Company will consider a phased return to work. This typically involves a gradual increase in working hours and/or duties over a period of **4 to 8 weeks**.

8.5.2 During a phased return, the employee will receive their normal salary for the hours worked. For any remaining hours in the first 4 weeks of the phased return, the Company will pay the employee their normal salary. After 4 weeks, any shortfall in hours may be covered by annual leave, unpaid leave, or a combination, as agreed between the employee and their manager.

8.5.3 The terms of a phased return will be agreed in writing before the return date and will be reviewed regularly.

### 8.6 Ill Health Retirement

In cases where an employee is unable to return to work and there is no reasonable prospect of recovery, the Company may consider ill health retirement. The employee may be eligible for benefits under the Company pension scheme, subject to the scheme rules and the recommendation of a medical adviser.

## 9. Occupational Health

9.1 The Company retains the services of an external Occupational Health provider, **OccuWell Ltd**, to provide medical advice and support in managing sickness absence.

9.2 Employees may be referred to Occupational Health by their line manager or the People & Culture Team. An employee's consent is required before a referral is made, though an unreasonable refusal to attend may be taken into account in any decisions made under this policy.

9.3 Occupational Health reports will be shared with the referring manager and the People & Culture Team. The employee will receive a copy of the report and will have the opportunity to comment on its contents.

9.4 Employees may also self-refer to Occupational Health by contacting the People & Culture Team.

## 10. Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)

10.1 The Company provides a confidential Employee Assistance Programme through **WellMind Solutions**, available to all employees and their immediate family members.

10.2 The EAP offers:
- 24/7 confidential telephone helpline: **0800 028 3456**
- Up to **6 sessions** of face-to-face or video counselling per issue per year
- Online resources covering mental health, financial wellbeing, legal advice, and family support
- Critical incident support

10.3 The EAP is entirely confidential. The Company is not informed of individual employees' use of the service, though anonymised usage data may be provided for monitoring purposes.

## 11. Absence Related to Disability

11.1 The Company recognises its obligations under the Equality Act 2010 and will make reasonable adjustments to support employees with disabilities to attend work.

11.2 Disability-related absences will be recorded separately from other sickness absences. They may be excluded from the trigger points in Section 6, depending on the circumstances. Each case will be considered individually, with advice from Occupational Health where appropriate.

11.3 Reasonable adjustments may include (but are not limited to):
- Modified duties or workload
- Adjusted working hours or patterns
- Provision of specialist equipment
- Working from home (where the role permits)
- Additional breaks
- Redeployment to a suitable alternative role

## 12. Absence During Pregnancy

Pregnancy-related sickness absence will be recorded separately and will not count towards the trigger points in Section 6. For full details, please refer to the **Parental Leave Policy (HR-PL-003)**, Section 8.

## 13. Annual Leave and Sickness

13.1 Employees continue to accrue annual leave during sickness absence in accordance with the **Annual Leave Policy (HR-AL-001)**.

13.2 An employee who falls ill while on annual leave may request that the period of illness be reclassified as sickness absence, provided they follow the notification procedures in Section 3 and provide appropriate medical certification.

13.3 Employees on long-term sick leave will be encouraged to take accrued annual leave during their absence. Any accrued but untaken leave at the end of the leave year will be carried over in accordance with statutory requirements.

## 14. Medical and Dental Appointments

14.1 Routine medical and dental appointments should, wherever possible, be arranged outside working hours or at the beginning or end of the working day. Where this is not possible, employees should give their line manager as much notice as possible.

14.2 Time off for routine appointments is not classed as sickness absence but should be recorded on PeopleHub. Please refer to the **Leave of Absence Policy (HR-LA-004)**, Section 6 for further details.

14.3 Time off for hospital appointments, specialist consultations, or ongoing treatment related to a medical condition will be treated sympathetically and will normally be granted as paid time off.

## 15. Data and Record Keeping

15.1 Sickness absence records are maintained on PeopleHub and will be processed in accordance with the Company's Data Protection Policy and the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR).

15.2 Sickness absence data will be monitored at team, department, and Company level to identify trends and support the development of health and wellbeing initiatives.

15.3 Individual sickness records will be retained for **6 years** after the end of the employee's employment.
