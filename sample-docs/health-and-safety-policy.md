## 1. Statement of Intent

1.1 Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") recognises its legal and moral obligation to provide a safe and healthy working environment for all employees, visitors, contractors, and anyone else who may be affected by our activities.

1.2 The Company is committed to:

- Providing and maintaining a safe working environment, with safe equipment and safe systems of work
- Ensuring that risks to health and safety are assessed, controlled, and regularly reviewed
- Providing adequate information, instruction, training, and supervision to enable all employees to work safely
- Consulting with employees on matters affecting their health and safety
- Continuously improving health and safety performance and management systems
- Complying with all applicable health and safety legislation, including the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974, the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999, and all associated regulations

1.3 This statement of intent is endorsed by the Chief Executive Officer and the Board of Directors.

## 3. Responsibilities

### 3.1 The Board of Directors

3.1.1 The Board has overall responsibility for health and safety within the Company and will ensure that:
- Adequate resources are allocated for health and safety management
- Health and safety performance is reviewed at Board level at least **quarterly**
- A competent Health & Safety Manager is appointed

### 3.2 The Chief Executive Officer

3.2.1 The CEO has ultimate accountability for health and safety and delegates day-to-day management to the Health & Safety Manager and line managers throughout the organisation.

### 3.3 The Health & Safety Manager

3.3.1 The Health & Safety Manager is **Tom Griffiths** (t.griffiths@meridiantech.co.uk, 0117 496 2270). The Health & Safety Manager is responsible for:

- Developing, implementing, and reviewing health and safety policies and procedures
- Conducting and coordinating risk assessments
- Investigating accidents and incidents
- Coordinating health and safety training
- Liaising with the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) and other enforcement bodies
- Maintaining accident and incident records
- Chairing the Health and Safety Committee

### 3.4 Line Managers and Team Leads

3.4.1 All managers and team leads are responsible for health and safety within their areas of control. This includes:

- Ensuring risk assessments are completed and communicated for their area
- Ensuring employees receive appropriate training and supervision
- Reporting hazards, accidents, and near-misses promptly
- Conducting regular workplace inspections (at least **monthly**)
- Ensuring that new starters receive a health and safety induction within their **first week**

### 3.5 All Employees

3.5.1 All employees have a duty under Section 7 of the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 to:

- Take reasonable care for their own health and safety and that of others
- Cooperate with the Company on health and safety matters
- Use equipment and substances in accordance with training and instructions
- Report any hazards, defects, accidents, or near-misses to their manager or the Health & Safety Manager immediately
- Not interfere with or misuse anything provided for health and safety purposes
- Attend all mandatory health and safety training

## 4. Risk Assessments

4.1 The Company will conduct suitable and sufficient risk assessments for all work activities, as required by the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999.

4.2 Risk assessments will be carried out:

- **Annually** for all routine activities and workplaces
- **After any accident, incident, or near-miss** to determine whether controls need to be revised
- **Before introducing new equipment, processes, or substances**
- **When there is a significant change** to work practices, staffing, or premises
- **On request** from any employee who has concerns about a particular risk

4.3 Risk assessments will be conducted by a competent person (the Health & Safety Manager, a trained assessor, or an external consultant) and will:

- Identify the hazards
- Determine who might be harmed and how
- Evaluate the risks and decide on control measures
- Record the significant findings
- Be communicated to all affected employees

4.4 Risk assessments will be reviewed at least **annually** or sooner if circumstances change.

4.5 All risk assessments are stored in the central Health & Safety folder on SharePoint and are accessible to all employees.

## 5. Fire Safety

### 5.1 General

5.1.1 The Company complies with the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005. A Fire Risk Assessment is conducted for each premises **annually** by a competent fire safety assessor.

5.1.2 The designated Responsible Person for fire safety at each location is the local Office Manager, supported by the Health & Safety Manager.

### 5.2 Fire Wardens

5.2.1 Trained Fire Wardens are appointed for each floor of every office. There are a minimum of **2 Fire Wardens per floor**. Fire Wardens are identified by high-visibility vests kept at their desks and by the list displayed next to each fire exit.

5.2.2 Fire Wardens are responsible for:
- Sweeping their designated area during an evacuation
- Guiding employees to the correct assembly point
- Reporting to the Chief Fire Warden that their area is clear
- Assisting individuals with mobility difficulties (Personal Emergency Evacuation Plans)

### 5.3 Evacuation Procedure

5.3.1 On hearing the fire alarm (continuous tone):

1. Stop work immediately and leave the building by the nearest safe exit
2. Do not stop to collect personal belongings
3. Do not use lifts
4. Close doors behind you where safe to do so
5. Proceed to the designated assembly point
6. Report to the Fire Warden for your area
7. Do not re-enter the building until the "all clear" is given by the Chief Fire Warden or the Fire and Rescue Service

### 5.4 Assembly Points

| Location | Assembly Point A | Assembly Point B |
|---|---|---|
| Bristol HQ | **Car Park A** (main visitor car park, north side) | **Car Park B** (staff overflow car park, south side) |
| London Office | **Lincolns Inn Fields** (north-west corner) | **Chancery Lane Gardens** |
| Manchester Office | **Stevenson Square** | **NCP Car Park entrance, Dale Street** |
| Edinburgh Office | **St Andrew Square Gardens** (east side) | **George Street pavement** (opposite side) |

5.4.1 Bristol HQ: Buildings 1 and 2 evacuate to **Car Park A**. Building 3 evacuates to **Car Park B**.

### 5.5 Fire Drills

5.5.1 Fire evacuation drills are conducted at least **twice per year** at each location. At least one drill per year will be unannounced. Drill results, including evacuation times, are recorded and reviewed. The target evacuation time is **4 minutes** for Bristol HQ and **3 minutes** for regional offices.

### 5.6 Fire Equipment

5.6.1 Fire extinguishers, fire blankets, and smoke detectors are provided throughout all premises and are inspected and maintained **annually** by a certified contractor. Quarterly visual checks are carried out by Fire Wardens.

## 6. First Aid

### 6.1 First Aiders

6.1.1 The Company maintains a sufficient number of trained first aiders in accordance with the Health and Safety (First-Aid) Regulations 1981.

6.1.2 There is a minimum of **1 trained first aider per floor** at each office location. The Bristol HQ has a minimum of **8 first aiders** across its three buildings.

6.1.3 First aiders hold a valid First Aid at Work (FAW) certificate (3-day course, renewed every **3 years**) or an Emergency First Aid at Work (EFAW) certificate (1-day course, renewed every **3 years**).

6.1.4 A list of current first aiders is displayed:
- Next to each first aid kit
- On the health and safety noticeboard on each floor
- On the Company intranet under Health & Safety > First Aid

### 6.2 First Aid Kit Locations

6.2.1 First aid kits are located in the following areas at each office:

- **Reception area** (main entrance)
- **Kitchen/break room** on each floor
- **Server room** (Bristol HQ only)
- **Warehouse/loading bay** (Bristol HQ Building 3)

6.2.2 First aid kits are checked and restocked **monthly** by the designated first aider for each area.

6.2.3 An Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is located in the reception area of each office. All first aiders and reception staff are trained in its use.

## 7. Accident Reporting

### 7.1 Internal Reporting

7.1.1 All accidents, injuries, near-misses, and dangerous occurrences must be reported to the employee's line manager and the Health & Safety Manager **within 24 hours** of the incident.

7.1.2 Reports should be submitted using the Accident Report Form available on the Company intranet (Health & Safety > Forms > Accident Report) or by emailing safety@meridiantech.co.uk.

7.1.3 The report should include:
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Name(s) of the injured person(s)
- Description of what happened
- Nature and extent of any injuries
- Names of any witnesses
- Immediate action taken

7.1.4 The Health & Safety Manager will investigate all reported incidents and determine whether corrective action is required.

### 7.2 RIDDOR Reporting

7.2.1 Certain serious incidents must be reported to the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) under the Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013 (RIDDOR). These include:

- Deaths
- Specified injuries (fractures, amputations, loss of sight, etc.)
- Injuries resulting in over **7 consecutive days** of incapacity for work
- Occupational diseases
- Dangerous occurrences (near-misses with potential for serious harm)

7.2.2 RIDDOR reports will be submitted by the Health & Safety Manager **within 10 days** of the incident (or 15 days for over-7-day injuries) via the HSE online reporting system at https://www.hse.gov.uk/riddor/.

7.2.3 Fatalities and specified injuries must be reported to the HSE **immediately by telephone** on 0345 300 9923.

## 8. Display Screen Equipment (DSE)

8.1 The Company complies with the Health and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations 1992 (as amended).

8.2 All employees who habitually use DSE as a significant part of their normal work are classified as "DSE users" and are entitled to:

### 8.1 DSE Assessments

8.1.1 A DSE workstation assessment will be carried out:
- Within **2 weeks** of starting employment or changing workstation
- Every **2 years** thereafter
- Following any change to equipment, furniture, or working arrangements
- On request, if the employee experiences discomfort

8.1.2 Assessments are conducted via the online DSE self-assessment tool on the intranet. The Health & Safety Manager will review completed assessments and arrange any necessary adjustments.

### 8.2 Eye Tests

8.2.1 DSE users are entitled to a free eye and eyesight test, paid for by the Company. The Company will contribute up to **£50** towards the cost of an eye test at any registered optician.

8.2.2 If the eye test shows that corrective lenses are needed specifically for DSE work (i.e., not for general use), the Company will contribute up to **£100** towards the cost of basic frames and lenses.

8.2.3 To claim, employees should submit a receipt and a copy of the prescription to the HR Department using the Eye Test Claim Form.

8.2.4 Eye tests may be repeated every **2 years**, or more frequently if recommended by the optician.

### 8.3 Breaks from DSE Work

8.3.1 Employees should take regular breaks from DSE work. The Company recommends a break of at least **5-10 minutes** away from the screen for every **50-60 minutes** of continuous DSE work. Breaks should involve a change of activity (e.g., filing, making a phone call, walking to a colleague's desk) rather than simply sitting idle.

## 9. Manual Handling

9.1 The Company will, so far as reasonably practicable, avoid the need for employees to undertake manual handling operations that involve a risk of injury.

9.2 Where manual handling cannot be avoided, a manual handling risk assessment will be carried out before the activity begins.

9.3 Employees involved in manual handling must receive appropriate training. Manual handling training is mandatory for all warehouse and facilities staff and will be refreshed every **2 years**.

9.4 Employees must not attempt to lift or move loads that they believe may cause injury. The general guideline maximum weight for a single-person lift is **25 kg for men** and **16 kg for women**, although these are guidelines only and individual capability, posture, and environment must be considered.

## 10. Lone Working

10.1 A lone worker is anyone who works by themselves without close or direct supervision, including employees working late, working from home, or visiting client sites alone.

10.2 Line managers must conduct a specific risk assessment for any employee who regularly works alone.

10.3 Lone workers must:
- Ensure someone knows where they are and when they expect to return
- Keep a mobile phone charged and accessible
- Follow the check-in procedure: contact their manager or a designated colleague at the start and end of lone working, and at agreed intervals (no longer than **2 hours** apart for site visits)
- Not undertake high-risk activities while working alone

10.4 The Company provides a lone worker safety app (StaySafe) for employees who regularly work alone off-site. The app provides GPS tracking and a panic button. Use of the app is mandatory for all employees who attend client sites alone.

## 11. Pregnant Workers and New Mothers

11.1 The Company will conduct an **additional specific risk assessment** for any employee who notifies the Company that she is pregnant, has given birth within the previous 6 months, or is breastfeeding.

11.2 The risk assessment will be conducted by the line manager with support from the Health & Safety Manager within **5 working days** of notification.

11.3 The assessment will consider, among other things:
- Manual handling
- Prolonged standing or sitting
- Exposure to chemicals or substances
- Stress and workload
- Workplace temperature
- Access to toilet facilities
- Working hours and night work

11.4 Where risks are identified, the Company will take reasonable steps to remove or reduce them, including adjusting working conditions, offering alternative work, or suspending the employee on full pay if no safe alternative is available.

11.5 A quiet, private room with a lockable door is available at each office for employees who need to breastfeed or express milk. At Bristol HQ, this is Room G.05 in Building 1. Contact the Facilities team or HR for room details at other locations.

## 12. Mental Health

12.1 The Company recognises that mental health is as important as physical health and is committed to promoting positive mental health and wellbeing in the workplace.

12.2 The Company has trained **Mental Health First Aiders (MHFAs)** at each location. There are currently **12 MHFAs** across the Company, with at least **2 per office location**.

12.3 Mental Health First Aiders are trained to:
- Recognise the signs of common mental health conditions
- Provide initial support and reassurance
- Guide individuals towards appropriate professional help
- Reduce stigma around mental health

12.4 A list of MHFAs is available on the Company intranet under Health & Safety > Mental Health.

12.5 The Company provides a confidential Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) through Health Assured, offering:
- **24/7 telephone counselling:** 0800 028 0199
- **Up to 6 sessions** of face-to-face or online counselling per issue per year
- Online CBT resources and wellbeing portal
- Legal and financial advice

12.6 Employees experiencing work-related stress should also speak to their line manager or HR. The Company will consider reasonable adjustments, workload reductions, and referral to Occupational Health where appropriate. See also the Company's Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-004).

## 13. Workplace Environment

### 13.1 Temperature

13.1.1 The Company will maintain a reasonable working temperature in all indoor workplaces. The minimum workplace temperature for office work is **16 degrees Celsius**, as recommended by the Approved Code of Practice to the Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992.

13.1.2 Where employees are engaged in physical work (e.g., warehouse), the minimum temperature is **13 degrees Celsius**.

13.1.3 During periods of hot weather, the Company will take reasonable steps to keep temperatures comfortable, including providing fans, relaxing dress code requirements, and providing cold drinking water. There is no legal maximum workplace temperature, but the Company aims to keep office temperatures below **26 degrees Celsius**.

13.1.4 Employees who are concerned about workplace temperature should report it to the Facilities team at facilities@meridiantech.co.uk.

### 13.2 Lighting

13.2.1 All workplaces will be adequately lit. Lighting levels will be assessed as part of the annual workplace risk assessment. Individual desk lamps are available on request from Facilities.

### 13.3 Noise

13.3.1 The Company will ensure that noise levels in the workplace do not exceed the lower exposure action value of **80 decibels** (daily or weekly average) as specified in the Control of Noise at Work Regulations 2005.

13.3.2 Noise levels are not typically a concern in office environments. However, the server room at Bristol HQ exceeds 80 dB, and hearing protection must be worn when entering. Hearing protection is provided at the server room entrance.

13.3.3 Open-plan offices are provided with acoustic panels and quiet rooms/phone booths for employees who need to concentrate or make calls without disturbance.

### 13.4 Ventilation

13.4.1 All enclosed workplaces are provided with adequate ventilation. Air conditioning and mechanical ventilation systems are maintained and serviced every **6 months**.

## 14. Smoking

14.1 In accordance with the Health Act 2006, smoking (including e-cigarettes and vaping) is **prohibited in all Company buildings**, including offices, meeting rooms, kitchens, toilets, stairwells, and car parks.

14.2 Smoking is also prohibited **within 5 metres** of any building entrance, window, or air intake.

14.3 Designated smoking areas are provided at each location, clearly marked with appropriate signage. At Bristol HQ, the designated smoking area is located at the far end of Car Park B, adjacent to the perimeter fence.

14.4 Employees who wish to stop smoking can access support through the Employee Assistance Programme (see Section 12.5) or the NHS Stop Smoking Service.
