# Meridian Technologies Ltd - Annual Leave Policy

**Policy Number:** HR-AL-001
**Version:** 3.2
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
**Review Date:** 1 January 2026
**Owner:** People & Culture Team
**Approved By:** Sarah Mitchell, Chief People Officer

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out the annual leave entitlements, booking procedures, and related provisions for all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company"). It applies to all employees regardless of grade or role, except where expressly stated otherwise for specific contract types.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd, including those on permanent, fixed-term, and probationary contracts. It does not apply to contractors, consultants, or agency workers, who should refer to the terms of their engagement.

For provisions relating to sickness absence, please refer to the **Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-002)**. For maternity, paternity, adoption, and shared parental leave, please refer to the **Parental Leave Policy (HR-PL-003)**. For compassionate leave, jury service, and other special leave, please refer to the **Leave of Absence Policy (HR-LA-004)**.

## 3. Leave Year

3.1 The annual leave year runs from **1 January to 31 December**.

3.2 Leave entitlement is calculated on the basis of the leave year. Employees who join or leave the Company part-way through the leave year will have their entitlement calculated on a pro-rata basis.

## 4. Annual Leave Entitlement

### 4.1 Full-Time Employees

Full-time employees (37.5 hours per week) are entitled to **25 days** of paid annual leave per leave year, in addition to the 8 UK public holidays listed in Section 10.

### 4.2 Long Service Entitlement

Employees accrue additional annual leave based on continuous service with the Company as follows:

| Continuous Service | Additional Days | Total Entitlement |
|---|---|---|
| Less than 3 years | 0 | 25 days |
| 3 to 5 years | 1 | 26 days |
| 5 to 10 years | 2 | 27 days |
| 10 years or more | 3 | 28 days |

Long service days are added to the entitlement at the start of the leave year in which the service milestone is reached.

### 4.3 Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees receive annual leave on a pro-rata basis calculated according to the number of hours or days worked per week. For example, an employee working 3 days per week (22.5 hours) would receive:

- Annual leave: 25 x (3/5) = **15 days**
- Public holidays: 8 x (3/5) = **4.8 days**

Part-time employees' public holiday entitlement is included in their overall leave allocation and must be booked in the usual way when a public holiday falls on one of their normal working days.

### 4.4 Accrual During First Year

Employees who join the Company part-way through the leave year will accrue annual leave at a rate of **2.08 days per complete calendar month** of service (equivalent to 25 days per annum). During the first 3 months of employment (probationary period), leave accrues but may only be taken with the prior written approval of your line manager.

## 5. Probationary Period

5.1 During the probationary period (normally 3 months, or 6 months for senior roles), annual leave will accrue as described in Section 4.4.

5.2 Employees may request to take accrued leave during the probationary period, but approval is at the discretion of their line manager and will take into account training schedules and business needs.

5.3 No more than **5 consecutive days** of annual leave may be taken during the probationary period unless exceptional circumstances apply and prior approval is given by the Head of Department.

## 6. Booking Annual Leave

### 6.1 Procedure

All annual leave requests must be submitted through the **PeopleHub HR system**. Verbal or informal requests (e.g., Slack messages) will not be accepted as formal leave bookings. Employees are responsible for ensuring their leave is properly recorded.

### 6.2 Notice Period

Employees must give notice of at least **twice the length of the leave requested**. For example:

| Leave Requested | Minimum Notice Required |
|---|---|
| 1 day | 2 working days |
| 3 days | 6 working days (approximately 1 week) |
| 5 days (1 week) | 10 working days (2 weeks) |
| 10 days (2 weeks) | 20 working days (4 weeks) |

For leave of 3 or more consecutive weeks, at least **6 weeks' notice** is required and approval from both the line manager and Head of Department is necessary.

### 6.3 Approval

Line managers will respond to leave requests within **3 working days** of submission. If no response is received within this period, employees should follow up with their manager directly. Leave should not be assumed to be approved until formal confirmation has been received via PeopleHub.

### 6.4 Team Coverage

Managers must ensure adequate team coverage at all times. As a general rule, no more than **one-third** of a team may be on annual leave at any given time. During peak periods (as defined by each department), further restrictions may apply. Managers will communicate any restricted periods at the start of each quarter.

### 6.5 Half Days

Employees may book annual leave in half-day increments. A half day is defined as 3.75 hours for full-time employees. Half days must be booked as either a morning (finishing at 12:30) or afternoon (starting at 13:00).

## 7. Cancellation of Annual Leave

### 7.1 Cancellation by the Employee

Employees may cancel approved annual leave provided they give the same notice period as required for the original booking (i.e., twice the length of the leave). Cancellation requests must be submitted through PeopleHub.

### 7.2 Cancellation by the Company

The Company reserves the right to cancel or postpone pre-approved annual leave in exceptional circumstances, such as critical project deadlines, major incidents, or unforeseen business requirements. The Company will reimburse any non-recoverable financial losses directly incurred as a result, subject to the employee providing reasonable evidence (e.g., flight or hotel booking confirmations). Claims must be submitted within 14 days of the cancellation.

## 8. Carry-Over of Annual Leave

8.1 Employees are encouraged to use their full annual leave entitlement within the leave year. A maximum of **5 days** of unused annual leave may be carried over to the following leave year.

8.2 Carried-over leave must be used by **31 March** of the following year. Any carried-over leave not taken by 31 March will be forfeited without payment.

8.3 In exceptional circumstances (e.g., where the Company has refused leave requests, or where the employee has been on long-term sickness absence), additional carry-over may be approved in writing by the Head of Department and the People & Culture Team.

8.4 Employees who have been absent on long-term sick leave should refer to the **Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-002)** for specific provisions regarding annual leave accrual during sickness absence.

## 9. Buying and Selling Annual Leave

### 9.1 Overview

The Company operates an annual leave purchase scheme, enabling employees to buy or sell up to **5 days** of annual leave per leave year. This scheme is available to employees who have completed their probationary period.

### 9.2 Buying Additional Leave

Employees may purchase up to 5 additional days of annual leave per year. The cost of each day is calculated by dividing the employee's annual basic salary by 260 (working days). The cost will be deducted from salary in equal monthly instalments over the remainder of the leave year.

### 9.3 Selling Annual Leave

Employees may sell up to 5 days of annual leave back to the Company, provided they retain a minimum of **20 days** of leave (plus public holidays) after the sale. Payment will be made at the same daily rate as described in Section 9.2 and will be subject to normal tax and National Insurance deductions.

### 9.4 Application Window

Applications to buy or sell leave must be submitted through PeopleHub during the annual enrolment window, which runs from **1 November to 30 November** each year for the following leave year. Applications outside this window will not normally be accepted.

## 10. Public Holidays

10.1 Full-time employees are entitled to **8 UK public holidays** per year in addition to their annual leave entitlement. The standard public holidays recognised by the Company are:

- New Year's Day
- Good Friday
- Easter Monday
- Early May Bank Holiday
- Spring Bank Holiday
- Summer Bank Holiday
- Christmas Day
- Boxing Day

10.2 Where a public holiday falls on a weekend, the Company will observe the government-designated substitute day.

10.3 Employees required to work on a public holiday will receive a day off in lieu, to be taken within 3 months of the public holiday. Alternatively, employees may request payment at **1.5 times** their normal daily rate, subject to line manager and budget approval.

10.4 Part-time employees' public holiday entitlement is calculated on a pro-rata basis as described in Section 4.3.

## 11. Annual Leave and Other Absences

11.1 If an employee falls ill during a period of annual leave, the absence may be reclassified as sickness absence, provided the employee follows the notification procedures set out in the **Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-002)** and provides a medical certificate (fit note) where applicable.

11.2 Annual leave continues to accrue during periods of maternity, paternity, adoption, and shared parental leave in accordance with the **Parental Leave Policy (HR-PL-003)**.

11.3 Annual leave continues to accrue during periods of sickness absence in line with statutory requirements.

## 12. Leaving the Company

12.1 When an employee's employment ends, their remaining annual leave entitlement will be calculated on a pro-rata basis up to and including their last day of employment.

12.2 If the employee has taken more leave than their pro-rata entitlement at the date of leaving, the Company will deduct the excess from the employee's final salary payment. The employee consents to this deduction by accepting this policy.

12.3 If the employee has unused leave remaining, the Company may either require the employee to take the outstanding leave during their notice period, or pay the employee in lieu of the outstanding leave.

12.4 Any leave purchased under the buy/sell scheme (Section 9) that has not been taken at the date of leaving will be adjusted in the final salary calculation.

## 13. Queries and Disputes

Any queries regarding annual leave entitlement or this policy should be directed to the People & Culture Team at **people@meridiantech.co.uk** or via PeopleHub. Disputes regarding leave will be handled through the Company's Grievance Procedure.
