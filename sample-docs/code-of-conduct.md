## 1. Introduction

1.1 This Code of Conduct sets out the standards of behaviour expected of all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company"). It reflects our values and the commitments we make to our clients, our colleagues, and the communities in which we operate.

1.2 This Code applies to all employees, officers, directors, contractors, and agency workers. It applies during working hours, at Company events, and whenever an individual is representing or could reasonably be perceived as representing the Company.

1.3 All employees are required to read and acknowledge this Code of Conduct within **14 days** of their start date and annually thereafter via the Company's HR portal. Failure to acknowledge does not exempt an employee from compliance.

1.4 Breaches of this Code may result in disciplinary action in accordance with the Company's **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure**, up to and including summary dismissal for serious breaches.

---

## 2. Our Values

2.1 Everything we do at Meridian Technologies is guided by four core values:

- **Integrity** - We are honest, transparent, and ethical in all our dealings
- **Excellence** - We set high standards and hold ourselves accountable
- **Collaboration** - We work together, respect different perspectives, and support one another
- **Innovation** - We embrace change, learn continuously, and challenge the status quo

2.2 Employees are expected to demonstrate these values in their day-to-day work, in their interactions with colleagues and clients, and in all decisions they make on behalf of the Company.

---

## 3. Professional Behaviour

3.1 All employees are expected to:

- Treat colleagues, clients, suppliers, and members of the public with dignity, courtesy, and respect
- Act honestly and with integrity at all times
- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and Company policies
- Perform their duties to the best of their ability and in accordance with their contract of employment
- Follow reasonable management instructions
- Raise concerns about wrongdoing through the appropriate channels (see the Company's **Whistleblowing Policy**)

3.2 The Company is committed to maintaining a workplace free from bullying, harassment, and discrimination of any kind. All employees have a responsibility to support this commitment. Complaints should be raised under the Company's **Dignity at Work Policy** or **Grievance Procedure**.

3.3 Intimidating, threatening, or violent behaviour will not be tolerated. Any employee who engages in or threatens violence in the workplace will face disciplinary action which may include summary dismissal. See Section 6.2 of the **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure** for the full list of gross misconduct offences.

## 4. Gifts and Hospitality

### 4.1 Receiving Gifts and Hospitality

4.1.1 Employees may accept modest gifts and hospitality where they are a normal and proportionate part of building business relationships. The maximum value of any gift that may be accepted is **£50**.

4.1.2 All gifts and hospitality with an estimated value exceeding **£25** must be recorded in the Company's Gifts and Hospitality Register within **5 working days** of receipt. The Register is maintained by the Compliance Team and is accessible via the Company intranet.

4.1.3 Employees must **decline** any gift or hospitality that:

- Exceeds £50 in value (unless pre-approved by a Director)
- Could reasonably be perceived as an attempt to influence a business decision
- Is in the form of cash or cash equivalents (vouchers, gift cards, etc.) of any value
- Is offered during a live procurement, tender, or contract negotiation process
- Comes from the same source more than **twice in any 12-month period**

4.1.4 Where an employee is unsure whether a gift or invitation is appropriate, they should consult their line manager or the Compliance Team **before** accepting.

### 4.2 Giving Gifts and Hospitality

4.2.1 Employees may offer gifts or hospitality to clients and business contacts in accordance with the limits set out in the **Expenses and Travel Policy** (Section 9). All gifts over **£25** in value must be recorded in the Gifts and Hospitality Register.

4.2.2 Gifts and hospitality must never be offered to public officials (including NHS staff, civil servants, and local government employees) without prior written approval from the Company's Legal Department.

4.2.3 The Company's obligations under the **Bribery Act 2010** apply to all gifts and hospitality. Employees must ensure that any gift or hospitality is proportionate, transparent, and given for a legitimate business purpose.

### 4.3 Annual Declaration

4.3.1 All employees at Grade 5 (Manager) and above are required to complete an annual Gifts and Hospitality Declaration by **31 January** each year, covering the preceding calendar year. This declaration must be submitted via the HR portal even if no gifts or hospitality were received.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

5.1 A conflict of interest arises where an employee's personal interests, relationships, or outside activities could, or could appear to, compromise their judgement, objectivity, or loyalty to the Company.

5.2 Employees must **declare any actual or potential conflict of interest** to their line manager and the HR Department as soon as they become aware of it, using the Conflict of Interest Declaration Form (available on the HR portal).

5.3 Common examples of conflicts of interest include (but are not limited to):

- A financial interest (including shares exceeding **1%** of issued capital) in a competitor, supplier, or client
- A close personal relationship with someone employed by a competitor, supplier, or client where that person is involved in dealings with the Company
- Involvement in a recruitment or procurement decision affecting a family member, partner, or close friend
- Sitting on the board of or providing advisory services to another organisation
- Operating a business that competes with or supplies services to the Company

5.4 The Company will assess each declaration and determine whether any action is needed, such as removing the employee from a particular decision-making process or requiring them to divest a financial interest.

5.5 All employees at Grade 5 (Manager) and above must complete an annual Conflict of Interest Declaration by **31 January** each year, even where no conflicts exist. This is submitted alongside the Gifts and Hospitality Declaration.

## 6. Outside Employment and Business Interests

6.1 Employees may undertake outside employment, freelance work, or business activities provided that:

- They obtain **prior written approval** from their line manager and the HR Department
- The activity does not conflict with the Company's interests or compete with its business
- The activity does not interfere with the employee's ability to perform their role, including compliance with the **Working Time Regulations 1998**
- The activity does not make use of Company equipment, premises, intellectual property, or confidential information

6.2 Approval for outside employment must be sought using the Outside Employment Request Form (available on the HR portal). Approval is granted for **12 months** and must be renewed annually.

6.3 The Company reserves the right to withdraw approval for outside employment at any time if it considers the activity to be detrimental to the Company's interests. Such withdrawal will be communicated in writing with reasons.

6.4 Employees who hold directorships or significant shareholdings in other companies must declare these under the Conflict of Interest process (Section 5).

## 7. Confidentiality and Data Protection

7.1 Employees must protect the confidentiality of all Company information, including client data, financial information, business strategies, product development plans, employee personal data, and any other information that is not in the public domain.

7.2 Confidential information must not be disclosed to any unauthorised person, whether inside or outside the Company, unless:

- Disclosure is required by law or regulation
- Disclosure is necessary for the proper performance of the employee's duties
- Prior written authorisation has been obtained from the Information Owner

7.3 The obligation of confidentiality continues after the termination of employment, as set out in the employee's contract of employment and any applicable restrictive covenants.

7.4 All employees must comply with the Company's **Data Protection Policy** and the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR). Breaches of data protection law may constitute gross misconduct (see the **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure**, Section 6.2).

7.5 If an employee becomes aware of an actual or suspected data breach, they must report it immediately to the Data Protection Officer at **dpo@meridiantech.co.uk** and to their line manager. Under UK GDPR, reportable breaches must be notified to the Information Commissioner's Office within **72 hours**.

## 8. Social Media and Public Communications

8.1 Employees must not publish any content on social media, blogs, forums, or other public platforms that:

- Purports to represent the views of the Company without prior written authorisation from the Marketing Department
- Discloses confidential Company information
- Could bring the Company into disrepute
- Is defamatory, discriminatory, threatening, or harassing towards any individual or organisation
- Discusses clients, projects, or internal matters in a way that could be identified

8.2 Employees are free to identify Meridian Technologies as their employer on professional networking sites (e.g., LinkedIn) and to share officially published Company content (press releases, blog posts, job vacancies). Personal opinions must be clearly identified as such and must include a disclaimer such as: *"Views are my own and do not represent my employer."*

8.3 Employees who are asked to contribute to Company social media accounts, blogs, or external publications must have prior written approval from the Marketing Department. Training on social media guidelines is available via the Company's learning management system.

8.4 The Company reserves the right to monitor publicly available social media content. Employees should be aware that social media posts may be visible to colleagues, clients, and competitors.

## 9. Dress Code and Appearance

9.1 The Company operates a **smart casual** dress code. Employees are expected to dress in a manner that is professional, clean, and appropriate for a business environment.

9.2 Smart casual includes (but is not limited to): collared shirts, blouses, smart jumpers, chinos, smart trousers, skirts, and clean shoes. T-shirts with inoffensive designs are acceptable on non-client-facing days.

9.3 On days when employees are meeting clients, attending client premises, or representing the Company externally, **business dress** is expected. This means suits, formal shirts/blouses, ties (optional), and formal footwear.

9.4 Employees working from home are expected to be presentable for video calls (see the **Remote and Hybrid Working Policy**, Section 7).

9.5 The Company will make reasonable adjustments to the dress code on religious, cultural, or medical grounds. Requests should be made to the HR Department.

9.6 Friday is designated as **casual dress day**, subject to no client meetings being scheduled.

## 10. Alcohol, Drugs, and Substance Misuse

10.1 Employees must not be under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs during working hours, including when working from home. **Zero tolerance** applies to illegal drug use in connection with employment.

10.2 Employees must not consume alcohol during working hours, including during lunch breaks, unless attending a Company-approved social event or client entertainment where moderate consumption is acceptable.

10.3 At Company social events where alcohol is available (e.g., team celebrations, summer parties, Christmas events):

- Employees are expected to drink responsibly and remain professional
- Employees remain subject to this Code of Conduct and all Company policies
- Managers have a duty of care and should intervene if an employee's behaviour becomes inappropriate
- The Company encourages the availability of non-alcoholic alternatives at all events

10.4 Employees who are concerned about their own or a colleague's alcohol or substance misuse are encouraged to seek confidential support through the Company's **Employee Assistance Programme** (EAP), available 24/7 on **0800 XXX XXXX**.

10.5 Employees taking prescribed medication that may affect their ability to work safely or effectively must inform their line manager (the nature of the medication need not be disclosed, only the potential effects). The Company's Occupational Health service is available for further guidance.

10.6 The Company reserves the right to conduct drug and alcohol testing in safety-critical roles or where there is reasonable cause to believe an employee is under the influence. Refusal to submit to a reasonable testing request may be treated as gross misconduct.

## 11. Personal Relationships at Work

11.1 The Company recognises that personal relationships may develop between colleagues. Such relationships are generally a private matter, but may need to be managed where they create, or could be perceived to create, a conflict of interest.

11.2 Employees **must declare** a personal relationship to their line manager and the HR Department where:

- One party has a direct or indirect reporting line to the other
- Both parties work in the same team or project
- One or both parties are involved in decisions that affect the other's employment (e.g., pay, promotion, performance reviews, disciplinary matters)
- The relationship is with an employee of a client, supplier, or competitor where it could influence business decisions

11.3 For the purposes of this policy, a "personal relationship" includes romantic relationships, cohabitation, marriage, civil partnership, and close family relationships (parent, sibling, child).

11.4 Where a declaration is made, the Company will consider whether any adjustments are needed, such as changes to reporting lines, project allocation, or involvement in decision-making. The Company will handle all declarations sensitively and with due regard for the privacy of the individuals involved.

11.5 Failure to declare a relevant personal relationship may result in disciplinary action, particularly where the undeclared relationship has influenced or could have influenced business decisions.

## 12. Use of Company Property and Resources

12.1 Company property, including IT equipment, software, office supplies, vehicles, and facilities, is provided for business purposes. Limited personal use of IT equipment (e.g., occasional personal emails, internet browsing during breaks) is permitted, provided it does not:

- Interfere with the employee's work or the work of others
- Incur costs to the Company
- Involve accessing inappropriate, illegal, or offensive material
- Breach any Company policy, including the **IT Acceptable Use Policy**

12.2 Company equipment must be returned in good condition upon termination of employment or when requested. Employees are responsible for the security and reasonable care of any Company property in their possession. See the **Remote and Hybrid Working Policy** for provisions relating to home-based equipment.

12.3 The Company monitors the use of its IT systems, including email, internet, and telephone usage, in accordance with its **IT Monitoring Policy**. Employees should not expect privacy when using Company systems.

12.4 Software must not be installed on Company equipment without authorisation from the IT Department. Unauthorised software may create security vulnerabilities and may breach licensing agreements.

## 13. Political Activities

13.1 Employees are free to engage in political activities in a personal capacity, including membership of political parties, campaigning, and standing for elected office.

13.2 Employees must not:

- Use Company resources (including email, premises, or equipment) for political activities
- Represent their political views as the views of the Company
- Allow political activities to interfere with their work or create conflict in the workplace
- Use their position at the Company to further political objectives

13.3 Employees who are elected to, or appointed to, public office (e.g., local council, school governor, magistrate) should inform their line manager and the HR Department. Reasonable time off may be granted in accordance with statutory provisions and the Company's **Time Off for Public Duties Policy**.

## 14. Reporting Concerns

14.1 Employees who witness or suspect a breach of this Code of Conduct should report it to:

- Their line manager, or
- The HR Department, or
- The Compliance Team at **compliance@meridiantech.co.uk**, or
- Through the Company's confidential **Whistleblowing Hotline** on **0800 XXX XXXX**

14.2 The Company will not tolerate retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith. Employees who believe they have been subjected to retaliation should report this through the same channels listed above or raise a formal complaint under the **Grievance Procedure**.

14.3 All reports will be treated confidentially to the extent possible and investigated promptly. The outcome of any investigation will be communicated to the reporting employee where appropriate, subject to data protection considerations.

---

## 15. Consequences of Breach

15.1 Breaches of this Code of Conduct will be addressed through the Company's **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure**. Depending on the severity and circumstances, consequences may range from informal coaching to summary dismissal.

15.2 Certain breaches (such as fraud, theft, violence, serious breach of confidentiality, or illegal drug use) may constitute gross misconduct and result in dismissal without notice. Refer to Section 6.2 of the **Disciplinary Policy and Procedure** for the full list.

15.3 Where a breach may also constitute a criminal offence, the Company reserves the right to report the matter to the relevant authorities.
