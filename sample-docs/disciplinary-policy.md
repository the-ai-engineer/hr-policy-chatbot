## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 This policy sets out the Company's procedure for managing disciplinary matters. It aims to ensure that all employees are treated fairly and consistently, and that any disciplinary action is proportionate, reasonable, and applied in accordance with the principles of natural justice.

1.2 This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") who have completed their probationary period. Employees within their probationary period are subject to a separate **Probationary Review Process** but may still be dismissed for gross misconduct under this policy.

1.3 This policy does not apply to:

- Contractors, consultants, or agency workers (who are subject to the terms of their engagement)
- Issues relating to poor performance or capability, which are addressed under the **Capability and Performance Management Policy**
- Complaints raised by employees about their own treatment, which are addressed under the **Grievance Procedure**

1.4 This policy has been drawn up in accordance with the **ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures** (2015). A failure by the Company to follow this procedure does not automatically render a dismissal unfair, but a tribunal may increase any compensation award by up to **25%** if the ACAS Code has not been followed without reasonable justification.

---

## 2. Principles

2.1 The Company is committed to the following principles in all disciplinary matters:

- **Fairness:** No disciplinary action will be taken until the matter has been fully investigated and the employee has had the opportunity to state their case.
- **Consistency:** The Company will apply this procedure consistently across all employees, taking into account individual circumstances.
- **Proportionality:** Any sanction imposed will be proportionate to the offence, taking into account the employee's disciplinary record, length of service, and any mitigating factors.
- **Timeliness:** The Company will deal with disciplinary matters without unreasonable delay.
- **Confidentiality:** All parties involved in a disciplinary process must treat the matter as confidential. Unauthorised disclosure may itself be treated as a disciplinary matter.
- **Right to be accompanied:** Employees have the statutory right to be accompanied at any formal disciplinary hearing (see Section 5.3).

---

## 3. Informal Resolution

3.1 Wherever possible and appropriate, the Company encourages the informal resolution of minor conduct issues before the formal disciplinary procedure is invoked.

3.2 Informal resolution may include:

- A private conversation between the line manager and the employee to discuss the concern
- An informal verbal reminder of expected standards
- Coaching, mentoring, or additional support
- Mediation (where the issue involves a relationship between colleagues)

3.3 An informal discussion is not a disciplinary sanction and will not be recorded on the employee's disciplinary record. However, the line manager may keep a brief personal note of the conversation for reference purposes.

3.4 If informal resolution does not achieve the required improvement, or if the conduct issue is too serious for informal handling, the formal procedure set out below will be followed.

## 4. Investigation

### 4.1 Purpose of Investigation

4.1.1 Before any formal disciplinary action is taken, the Company will conduct a reasonable investigation to establish the facts. The purpose of the investigation is to determine whether there is a case to answer, not to determine guilt or innocence.

### 4.2 Appointment of Investigating Officer

4.2.1 The Company will appoint an **Investigating Officer**, who will normally be the employee's line manager or another manager at an appropriate level. For serious matters or where the line manager is involved, the HR Department will appoint an alternative Investigating Officer.

4.2.2 The Investigating Officer must not be the same person who chairs any subsequent disciplinary hearing, to ensure impartiality.

### 4.3 Investigation Process

4.3.1 The investigation may include:

- Reviewing relevant documents, records, emails, and CCTV footage
- Interviewing the employee under investigation
- Interviewing witnesses (who may be accompanied by a colleague or trade union representative if they wish)
- Seeking specialist advice (e.g., IT forensics, Occupational Health)

4.3.2 The employee will be informed that an investigation is taking place and the general nature of the allegation(s). They will be given the opportunity to respond, although they are not obliged to attend an investigatory interview.

4.3.3 Investigations will normally be completed within **15 working days**. Where this is not possible (e.g., due to the complexity of the matter or the availability of witnesses), the employee will be kept informed of the progress and the reasons for any delay.

### 4.4 Investigation Outcome

4.4.1 At the conclusion of the investigation, the Investigating Officer will prepare a written report setting out the findings and recommending one of the following:

- **No further action:** The allegation is not substantiated, and the matter is closed
- **Informal resolution:** The matter should be dealt with informally (see Section 3)
- **Formal disciplinary hearing:** There is a case to answer, and the matter should proceed to a formal hearing

## 5. Formal Disciplinary Hearing

### 5.1 Notice of Hearing

5.1.1 Where the investigation recommends a formal hearing, the employee will be notified in writing at least **5 working days** before the hearing date. The notification will include:

- The specific allegation(s) and the policy or standard that has allegedly been breached
- A summary of the evidence gathered during the investigation
- Copies of any documents that will be referred to at the hearing
- The names of any witnesses the Company intends to call
- The potential consequences if the allegation(s) are upheld (including, where applicable, that dismissal is a possible outcome)
- The employee's right to be accompanied (see Section 5.3)
- The date, time, and location of the hearing

5.1.2 If the employee is unable to attend on the proposed date, they should inform the HR Department as soon as possible. The employee may propose an alternative date, which must be within **5 working days** of the original date. If the employee fails to attend without good reason on two occasions, the hearing may proceed in their absence.

### 5.2 Conduct of the Hearing

5.2.1 The hearing will be chaired by a manager (the "Hearing Manager") who has not been involved in the investigation. For hearings where dismissal is a potential outcome, the Hearing Manager will normally be at Director level or above.

5.2.2 The HR Department will attend in an advisory capacity. A note-taker will also be present to produce a written record of the hearing.

5.2.3 The hearing will follow this general format:

1. The Hearing Manager will explain the purpose of the hearing and the procedure to be followed
2. The Investigating Officer will present the management case, including evidence and witnesses
3. The employee (and their companion) will have the opportunity to question the management witnesses
4. The employee will present their response, including any evidence and witnesses
5. The management side will have the opportunity to question the employee's witnesses
6. Both sides will have the opportunity to summarise their case
7. The Hearing Manager will adjourn to consider the evidence and reach a decision

5.2.4 The Hearing Manager may adjourn the hearing at any point if further investigation is needed.

### 5.3 Right to be Accompanied

5.3.1 Employees have the statutory right to be accompanied at any formal disciplinary hearing by:

- A **trade union representative** (who may be an official employed by the union or a workplace representative), or
- A **work colleague** employed by the Company

5.3.2 The companion may address the hearing, confer with the employee, and sum up the employee's case, but may not answer questions on the employee's behalf.

5.3.3 If the employee's chosen companion is unavailable on the proposed hearing date, the employee may request a postponement. The Company will accommodate a reasonable alternative date within **5 working days** of the original date.

5.3.4 The Company does not permit legal representatives to attend disciplinary hearings, except in cases where criminal proceedings are also being pursued and the Company agrees that legal representation is appropriate.

## 6. Disciplinary Sanctions

### 6.1 Stages of Disciplinary Action

6.1.1 Disciplinary action will normally follow a progressive series of stages. However, the Company reserves the right to enter the procedure at any stage, including dismissal, depending on the severity of the misconduct.

| Stage | Sanction | Duration on Record |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 | Verbal Warning | **6 months** from date of issue |
| Stage 2 | First Written Warning | **6 months** from date of issue |
| Stage 3 | Final Written Warning | **12 months** from date of issue |
| Stage 4 | Dismissal with notice | Permanent record |

6.1.2 **Verbal Warning (Stage 1):** Issued for minor misconduct where informal resolution has not been effective or is not appropriate. The employee will be told what improvement is expected and the timeframe for improvement. The warning will be confirmed in writing within **5 working days** of the hearing.

6.1.3 **First Written Warning (Stage 2):** Issued for more serious misconduct, or for a repetition of conduct that was the subject of a verbal warning. The warning letter will set out the nature of the misconduct, the expected standards, and the consequences of further misconduct within the warning period.

6.1.4 **Final Written Warning (Stage 3):** Issued for serious misconduct, or for a repetition of conduct that was the subject of a first written warning. The warning letter will make clear that any further misconduct within the warning period is likely to result in dismissal.

6.1.5 **Dismissal (Stage 4):** The Company may dismiss the employee with their contractual or statutory notice period (whichever is greater) where:

- The employee's conduct has not improved following a final written warning
- The misconduct is sufficiently serious to warrant dismissal but does not constitute gross misconduct

### 6.2 Gross Misconduct

6.2.1 Gross misconduct is misconduct so serious that it fundamentally undermines the employment relationship, justifying **summary dismissal** (i.e., dismissal without notice or payment in lieu of notice).

6.2.2 The following are examples of conduct that will normally be regarded as gross misconduct. This list is not exhaustive:

- **Theft, fraud, or dishonesty** - including falsification of Company records, timesheets, or expense claims
- **Physical violence or assault** - including threats of violence towards any person on Company premises or in the course of employment
- **Serious harassment, bullying, or discrimination** - including sexual harassment, racial harassment, or any conduct that violates the **Dignity at Work Policy**
- **Serious breach of data security or confidentiality** - including unauthorised disclosure of Company, client, or personal data; deliberate breach of the **Data Protection Policy** or **Information Security Policy** (see also Section 7 of the **Code of Conduct** and Section 9 of the **Remote and Hybrid Working Policy**)
- **Being under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs** during working hours or on Company premises (see Section 10 of the **Code of Conduct**)
- **Possession, use, or distribution of illegal drugs** on Company premises or during working hours
- **Serious insubordination** - including a deliberate and wilful refusal to follow a reasonable management instruction
- **Serious negligence** that causes, or could cause, significant harm, loss, or damage to the Company, its employees, clients, or property
- **Deliberate damage** to Company property or the property of colleagues, clients, or visitors
- **Bringing the Company into serious disrepute** - including criminal conduct outside of work that affects the employee's suitability for their role or damages the Company's reputation
- **Serious breach of health and safety rules** that endangers the employee or others
- **Bribery or corruption** - including offering, soliciting, or accepting bribes in breach of the **Bribery Act 2010** and the Company's **Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy**
- **Serious breach of the Company's IT Acceptable Use Policy** - including accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal, pornographic, or offensive material using Company systems
- **Unauthorised absence** from work without notification for **3 or more consecutive working days**

6.2.3 Where gross misconduct is alleged, the employee will normally be suspended pending investigation (see Section 7). If gross misconduct is proven at a disciplinary hearing, the employee will be summarily dismissed. The employee will be paid for any accrued but untaken annual leave.

## 7. Suspension

7.1 The Company may suspend an employee on **full pay** during a disciplinary investigation where:

- The allegation(s) involve potential gross misconduct
- The employee's continued presence in the workplace could compromise the investigation (e.g., risk of tampering with evidence or influencing witnesses)
- There is a risk to other employees, clients, or Company property

7.2 Suspension is a **neutral act** and is not a disciplinary sanction. It does not imply guilt or that any particular outcome has been predetermined.

7.3 Suspension will be confirmed in writing within **2 working days**, setting out:

- The reason(s) for the suspension
- The expected duration (or an indication that the employee will be kept informed)
- The terms of the suspension (e.g., requirement to remain available during working hours, restrictions on contacting colleagues, requirement to return Company property)
- The name of the employee's point of contact during the suspension (normally an HR representative)

7.4 Suspension will be kept as **short as reasonably practicable** and will be reviewed by the HR Department at least every **2 weeks**. The Company recognises the detrimental effect that prolonged suspension can have on an employee's wellbeing and will make the Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) available.

7.5 During suspension, the employee:

- Will receive their normal basic salary and contractual benefits
- Must remain available during normal working hours and be contactable by telephone or email
- Must not attend the workplace unless specifically requested
- Must not contact colleagues or clients about the matter under investigation (other than their chosen companion for any hearing)
- Must return any Company property as requested (laptop, access badge, etc.)
- May continue to access the Company's Employee Assistance Programme

## 8. Appeals

### 8.1 Right of Appeal

8.1.1 An employee has the right to appeal against any formal disciplinary sanction, including dismissal.

8.1.2 Appeals must be submitted **in writing** to the HR Department within **5 working days** of receiving the written confirmation of the disciplinary decision. The appeal should clearly state the grounds for appeal, which may include:

- The employee believes the procedure was not applied correctly
- The employee believes the decision was not supported by the evidence
- The employee believes the sanction was disproportionate
- New evidence has come to light that was not available at the original hearing
- The employee believes there were mitigating circumstances that were not adequately considered

### 8.2 Appeal Hearing

8.2.1 The appeal will be heard by a **senior manager** who has not previously been involved in the case. For appeals against dismissal, the appeal will normally be heard by a Director or the CEO.

8.2.2 The appeal hearing will be arranged within **10 working days** of receipt of the appeal, wherever practicable. The employee will receive at least **5 working days' notice** of the hearing date.

8.2.3 The employee has the right to be accompanied at the appeal hearing on the same basis as the original hearing (see Section 5.3).

8.2.4 The appeal hearing may be a **review of the original decision** or a **full rehearing** of the case, at the discretion of the Appeal Manager. The Appeal Manager will determine the appropriate approach based on the grounds of appeal.

### 8.3 Appeal Outcome

8.3.1 The Appeal Manager may:

- **Uphold the original decision** and sanction
- **Uphold the original decision but reduce the sanction** (e.g., substitute a final written warning for dismissal)
- **Overturn the original decision** (e.g., find that the misconduct is not proven and revoke the sanction)
- **Order a new hearing** before a different Hearing Manager if procedural deficiencies are identified

8.3.2 The outcome of the appeal will be communicated to the employee in writing within **5 working days** of the appeal hearing. The decision of the Appeal Manager is **final** and there is no further right of internal appeal.

8.3.3 Where a dismissal is overturned on appeal, the employee will be reinstated with no break in continuity of service, and any pay withheld during the appeal period will be restored.

## 9. Record Keeping and Data Retention

9.1 A written record will be kept of all formal disciplinary proceedings, including:

- The allegation(s) and the investigation report
- Notes from the disciplinary hearing and any appeal hearing
- The decision and reasons
- Any sanction imposed and its duration

9.2 Disciplinary records will be retained in accordance with the following schedule:

| Sanction | Retention Period (on personnel file) |
|---|---|
| Verbal Warning | **6 months** from date of issue, then removed |
| First Written Warning | **6 months** from date of issue, then removed |
| Final Written Warning | **12 months** from date of issue, then removed |
| Dismissal | **6 years** from date of termination |
| Gross Misconduct Dismissal | **6 years** from date of termination |

9.3 "Removed" means the warning will be disregarded for disciplinary purposes and removed from the employee's active personnel file. The Company may retain a confidential archive record for the purpose of defending any legal claims, subject to the requirements of the UK GDPR.

9.4 Spent warnings will not normally be taken into account in future disciplinary proceedings, except in exceptional circumstances where the earlier conduct is directly relevant to a new allegation and a Director authorises its consideration.

9.5 Employees have the right to access their personnel file, including disciplinary records, in accordance with their rights under the UK GDPR. Subject access requests should be made to the Data Protection Officer at **dpo@meridiantech.co.uk**.

## 10. Overlap with Other Policies and Procedures

10.1 **Grievance Procedure:** Where an employee raises a grievance during a disciplinary process, the Company will consider whether the disciplinary process should be paused while the grievance is investigated, or whether both processes can run concurrently. This decision will be communicated to the employee in writing.

10.2 **Capability and Performance Management Policy:** Where it is unclear whether a matter is a conduct issue (dealt with under this policy) or a performance issue (dealt with under the Capability Policy), the HR Department will advise on the appropriate procedure. In general:

- **Misconduct** = "won't do" (the employee chooses not to meet the required standard)
- **Capability** = "can't do" (the employee is unable to meet the standard despite reasonable support)

10.3 **Dignity at Work Policy:** Allegations of bullying, harassment, or discrimination may be investigated under this policy or the Dignity at Work Policy, depending on the circumstances. The HR Department will advise on the appropriate procedure.

10.4 **Code of Conduct:** Breaches of the **Code of Conduct** may give rise to disciplinary action under this policy. In particular, Sections 4 (Gifts and Hospitality), 7 (Confidentiality), 10 (Alcohol and Drugs), and 12 (Company Property) of the Code set out standards whose breach may constitute misconduct or gross misconduct.

10.5 **Remote and Hybrid Working Policy:** Breaches of data security requirements while working remotely (Section 9 of the Remote Working Policy) may constitute gross misconduct under this policy (see Section 6.2 above).

10.6 **Expenses and Travel Policy:** Fraudulent expense claims constitute gross misconduct (see Section 2.4 of the Expenses Policy and Section 6.2 of this policy).

## 11. Special Circumstances

### 11.1 Criminal Charges or Convictions

11.1.1 Where an employee is charged with or convicted of a criminal offence, the Company will consider the effect on the employee's suitability for their role, the impact on the business, and the nature of the offence. A criminal charge or conviction will not automatically result in disciplinary action, but the Company may commence disciplinary proceedings where the conduct is relevant to the employment relationship.

11.1.2 The Company may proceed with its own investigation and disciplinary process regardless of any criminal investigation or proceedings. The Company is not required to wait for the outcome of criminal proceedings before taking disciplinary action.

### 11.2 Trade Union Representatives

11.2.1 Where disciplinary action is being considered against a trade union representative, the Company will inform the relevant full-time trade union official at the earliest opportunity (with the employee's consent) and before any formal action is taken.

### 11.3 Employees on Long-Term Absence

11.3.1 Where an employee is on long-term sickness absence and a disciplinary matter arises, the Company will take all reasonable steps to enable the employee to participate in the process, including:

- Conducting hearings at an alternative venue
- Allowing the employee to participate by video conference
- Considering written submissions where attendance is not possible
- Seeking Occupational Health advice on the employee's fitness to participate

## 12. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Line Manager | Addresses minor conduct issues informally; may act as Investigating Officer for routine matters; refers serious matters to HR |
| Investigating Officer | Conducts the investigation fairly and objectively; prepares the investigation report |
| Hearing Manager | Chairs the disciplinary hearing; makes the decision on outcome and sanction |
| Appeal Manager | Hears the appeal; makes the final decision |
| HR Department | Advises on the application of this policy; ensures consistency and compliance; provides administrative support; manages records |
| Employee | Co-operates with the investigation and disciplinary process; attends meetings as required; raises any concerns or mitigating factors |
