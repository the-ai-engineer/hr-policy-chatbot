## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the entitlements and procedures for employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") who are expecting or adopting a child, or whose partner is expecting or adopting a child. The Company is committed to supporting employees through this significant life event and aims to offer provisions that exceed the statutory minimum where possible.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees of Meridian Technologies Ltd. Eligibility for specific entitlements varies by leave type and is set out in the relevant sections below.

For provisions relating to annual leave accrued during parental leave, please refer to the **Annual Leave Policy (HR-AL-001)**, Section 11.2. For time off for dependants in emergency situations, please refer to the **Leave of Absence Policy (HR-LA-004)**.

## 3. Maternity Leave

### 3.1 Entitlement

All pregnant employees are entitled to up to **52 weeks** of maternity leave, regardless of length of service. This comprises:

- **Ordinary Maternity Leave (OML):** The first 26 weeks
- **Additional Maternity Leave (AML):** The following 26 weeks

There is no minimum service requirement for the right to take maternity leave.

### 3.2 Maternity Pay

Eligible employees will receive maternity pay as follows:

| Period | Pay |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1 to 16 | **Full pay** (basic salary) |
| Weeks 17 to 39 | **Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP)** -- currently £184.03 per week or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower (2025/26 rate) |
| Weeks 40 to 52 | **Unpaid** |

**Enhanced maternity pay** (weeks 1-16 at full pay) is available to employees who have completed at least **12 months' continuous service** by the 15th week before the Expected Week of Childbirth (EWC). Employees with less than 12 months' service will receive:
- Weeks 1 to 6: 90% of average weekly earnings
- Weeks 7 to 39: SMP at the standard rate
- Weeks 40 to 52: Unpaid

Enhanced maternity pay is inclusive of SMP. It is not paid in addition to SMP.

### 3.3 Notification Requirements

Employees must notify the Company of their pregnancy **no later than the 15th week before the EWC** (i.e., by the end of the 25th week of pregnancy). The notification must include:

(a) Confirmation that they are pregnant
(b) The Expected Week of Childbirth, supported by a **MAT B1 certificate** (usually available from the 20th week of pregnancy)
(c) The date on which they intend to start maternity leave

Notification should be given in writing to the line manager and the People & Culture Team. The Company will respond in writing within **28 days**, confirming the expected end date of the maternity leave.

### 3.4 When Maternity Leave Can Start

Maternity leave may start at any time from the **11th week before the EWC** (i.e., from week 29 of pregnancy). It cannot start earlier than this date.

Maternity leave will start automatically if:
- The employee is absent from work due to a pregnancy-related illness in the **4 weeks before the EWC** (from week 36 of pregnancy)
- The baby is born before the planned start date

### 3.5 Changing the Start Date

The employee may bring forward or postpone the maternity leave start date by giving at least **28 days' written notice** before the new start date (or the original start date, whichever is earlier).

## 4. Antenatal Appointments

4.1 Pregnant employees are entitled to **paid time off** to attend antenatal appointments as recommended by their doctor, midwife, or health visitor. This includes medical examinations, relaxation classes, and parent-craft classes where recommended by a medical professional.

4.2 Employees should provide their line manager with reasonable notice of antenatal appointments and, if requested, show evidence of the appointment (e.g., appointment card or letter).

4.3 Partners of pregnant women are entitled to take **unpaid time off** to accompany them to up to **2 antenatal appointments**, each lasting a maximum of 6.5 hours including travel time. However, the Company will exercise discretion and will normally grant this as **paid time off**.

## 5. Paternity Leave

### 5.1 Eligibility

Paternity leave is available to employees who:
- Have at least **26 weeks' continuous service** by the 15th week before the EWC (or by the week in which the adopter is matched with a child)
- Are the biological father of the child, or the partner (including same-sex partner) of the mother/primary adopter
- Have responsibility for the child's upbringing

### 5.2 Entitlement

Eligible employees are entitled to **2 weeks** of paternity leave at **full pay** (basic salary).

Paternity leave must be taken in a block of either 1 week or 2 consecutive weeks. It cannot be taken as individual days.

### 5.3 Timing

Paternity leave must be taken within **56 days (8 weeks)** of the birth of the child (or placement for adoption).

### 5.4 Notification

Employees must give at least **15 weeks' notice** before the EWC of their intention to take paternity leave, using the Company's Paternity Leave Request Form (available on PeopleHub). The employee must confirm:
- The EWC or expected placement date
- Whether they wish to take 1 or 2 weeks
- When they want the leave to start

The employee may change the start date by giving at least **28 days' notice**.

## 6. Shared Parental Leave (SPL)

### 6.1 Overview

Shared Parental Leave allows eligible parents to share up to **50 weeks of leave** and up to **37 weeks of pay** between them. It provides flexibility by enabling both parents to take leave at the same time or at different times.

### 6.2 Eligibility

To qualify for SPL, the employee must:
- Have at least 26 weeks' continuous service by the 15th week before the EWC
- Share responsibility for the child with the other parent
- The mother/primary adopter must have curtailed (or given notice to curtail) their maternity/adoption leave

The other parent must also meet certain employment and earnings conditions.

### 6.3 How SPL Works

The mother/primary adopter must take at least **2 weeks** of compulsory maternity/adoption leave. The remaining leave (up to 50 weeks) can be shared between the parents.

For example, if the mother takes 20 weeks of maternity leave and then returns to work, the remaining 32 weeks could be taken by the partner as SPL (or shared between them in whatever pattern they choose).

### 6.4 SPL Pay

Shared Parental Pay (ShPP) is paid at the statutory rate of **£184.03 per week** (or 90% of average weekly earnings if lower) for up to 37 weeks, minus any weeks of SMP or SAP already taken by the mother/primary adopter.

The Company does not currently enhance ShPP beyond the statutory rate.

### 6.5 Notification

Employees must give at least **8 weeks' written notice** before each period of SPL. Up to **3 separate notifications** (requests for blocks of leave) may be made, though the Company will consider additional requests sympathetically.

Each notification should include:
- The start and end dates of the SPL requested
- An indication of the expected pattern of leave
- A signed declaration from both parents

### 6.6 Continuous vs Discontinuous Leave

A request for a single continuous block of SPL will be automatically approved. A request for discontinuous leave (e.g., alternating blocks between parents) may be refused by the Company. If refused, the employee may take the total amount of SPL requested as a single continuous block, starting on the date of the first period requested.

## 7. Adoption Leave

### 7.1 Entitlement

Employees who are adopting a child are entitled to up to **52 weeks** of adoption leave, mirroring the structure of maternity leave:

- **Ordinary Adoption Leave (OAL):** The first 26 weeks
- **Additional Adoption Leave (AAL):** The following 26 weeks

Only one member of a couple may take adoption leave. The other may be eligible for paternity leave (Section 5) or shared parental leave (Section 6).

### 7.2 Eligibility

To qualify for adoption leave, the employee must:
- Have been **matched with a child** for adoption by an approved adoption agency
- Have at least **26 weeks' continuous service** by the week in which they are notified of the match

Adoption leave is not available for step-parent adoptions or adoptions by existing foster parents where there is no change in the child's home.

### 7.3 Adoption Pay

Eligible employees will receive adoption pay as follows:

| Period | Pay |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1 to 16 | **Full pay** (basic salary) |
| Weeks 17 to 39 | **Statutory Adoption Pay (SAP)** -- currently £184.03 per week or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower |
| Weeks 40 to 52 | **Unpaid** |

Enhanced adoption pay (weeks 1-16 at full pay) is available to employees with at least **12 months' continuous service** at the date of the match, on the same terms as enhanced maternity pay (Section 3.2).

### 7.4 Notification

The employee must notify the Company of their intention to take adoption leave within **7 days** of being notified of the match, providing:
- The date of the match
- The expected date of the child's placement
- The date on which they wish to start adoption leave

### 7.5 Pre-Adoption Appointments

Employees who are the primary adopter are entitled to paid time off for up to **5 pre-adoption appointments**. The secondary adopter is entitled to unpaid time off for up to **2 pre-adoption appointments** (though the Company will normally grant this as paid time off).

## 8. Pregnancy-Related Illness

8.1 Absence due to pregnancy-related illness will be recorded separately from other sickness absence and will not be counted towards the trigger points in the **Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-002)**, Section 6.

8.2 If a pregnancy-related illness occurs in the **4 weeks before the EWC**, maternity leave will be triggered automatically (see Section 3.4).

8.3 Employees who suffer a miscarriage **before the 25th week of pregnancy** will be entitled to sick leave in accordance with the **Sickness Absence Policy (HR-SA-002)**. The Company will treat such cases with sensitivity and will not apply absence triggers.

8.4 Employees who suffer a stillbirth **after the 24th week of pregnancy** are entitled to full maternity leave and pay.

## 9. Keeping in Touch (KIT) Days

### 9.1 Maternity and Adoption Leave

Employees on maternity or adoption leave may work (or attend training, team meetings, or other activities) for up to **10 Keeping in Touch (KIT) days** without bringing their maternity or adoption leave to an end.

9.2 KIT days are optional. Neither the Company nor the employee is obliged to agree to them. Work must not be carried out during the **2-week compulsory maternity leave period** immediately following the birth.

9.3 Employees will be paid their **normal daily rate** for each KIT day worked, offset against any SMP, SAP, or enhanced pay received for that week. The minimum amount payable for a KIT day is one day's normal pay.

### 9.4 Shared Parental Leave in Touch (SPLIT) Days

Employees on Shared Parental Leave may work for up to **20 SPLIT days** without bringing their SPL to an end. The same principles as KIT days apply.

## 10. Terms and Conditions During Leave

10.1 During maternity, paternity, adoption, or shared parental leave, the employee's contract of employment continues and they are entitled to the benefit of all their contractual terms and conditions (except remuneration), including:

- Annual leave accrual (see **Annual Leave Policy (HR-AL-001)**, Section 11.2)
- Pension contributions (during paid leave only -- employer contributions continue based on normal salary; employee contributions are based on actual pay received)
- Company benefits (private medical insurance, life assurance, etc.)

10.2 Time spent on maternity, adoption, or shared parental leave counts towards the employee's continuous service for all purposes.

## 11. Right to Return

### 11.1 Return from Ordinary Maternity/Adoption Leave (first 26 weeks)

Employees returning from OML or OAL are entitled to return to the **same job** on the same terms and conditions as if they had not been absent.

### 11.2 Return from Additional Maternity/Adoption Leave (weeks 27-52)

Employees returning from AML or AAL are entitled to return to the **same job**, or if that is not reasonably practicable, to a **suitable and appropriate alternative role** on terms and conditions no less favourable than the original role.

### 11.3 Returning Early

An employee who wishes to return to work before the end of their full leave entitlement must give at least **8 weeks' written notice** of their intended return date. If insufficient notice is given, the Company may postpone the return date to ensure 8 weeks' notice is provided (but not beyond the end of the leave period).

### 11.4 Flexible Working

Employees returning from parental leave who wish to change their working pattern (e.g., part-time working, compressed hours, or working from home) should submit a **flexible working request** in writing to the People & Culture Team at least 8 weeks before their intended return date. The Company will consider all such requests seriously and in accordance with the statutory flexible working procedure.

## 12. Ordinary Parental Leave (Unpaid)

### 12.1 Entitlement

In addition to the provisions above, employees with at least **1 year's continuous service** are entitled to take up to **18 weeks' unpaid parental leave** for each child, up to the child's 18th birthday. This is a separate statutory entitlement.

### 12.2 Conditions

- Leave must be taken in blocks of **complete weeks** (except for parents of disabled children, who may take leave in individual days)
- A maximum of **4 weeks** may be taken in any one year per child
- At least **21 days' notice** must be given
- The Company may postpone the leave for up to 6 months if the business would be particularly disrupted, except where the leave is requested immediately after the birth or placement of a child

## 13. Neonatal Care Leave

13.1 Where a newborn baby requires neonatal care (admission to hospital within 28 days of birth for a continuous period of 7 or more days), eligible employees are entitled to up to **12 weeks of paid neonatal care leave** in addition to their other parental leave entitlements.

13.2 Neonatal care leave is available to all employees from day one of employment. Statutory Neonatal Care Pay is available to employees who have at least 26 weeks' continuous service and meet the earnings threshold.

13.3 The Company will enhance neonatal care pay to **full pay** for the duration of the neonatal care leave, for employees with at least 12 months' continuous service.

## 14. Bereavement

14.1 Employees who suffer the loss of a child under 18 (including stillbirth after 24 weeks of pregnancy) are entitled to **2 weeks' paid parental bereavement leave** under Jack's Law. This leave must be taken within 56 weeks of the death.

14.2 The Company will also consider compassionate leave in these circumstances. Please refer to the **Leave of Absence Policy (HR-LA-004)**, Section 3.
