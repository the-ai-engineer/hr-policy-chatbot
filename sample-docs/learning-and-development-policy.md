## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1 Meridian Technologies Ltd ("the Company") is committed to the continuous professional development of all employees. We believe that investing in our people is essential to delivering excellent work, retaining talent, and achieving our business objectives.

1.2 This policy sets out the learning and development opportunities available to employees and the processes for accessing them.

1.3 This policy applies to all permanent employees of the Company. Employees on fixed-term contracts of 12 months or more are eligible on the same basis. Employees within their probation period are eligible for mandatory training only; discretionary development is available after successful completion of probation (see Probation Policy HR-PR-011).

1.4 Agency workers, consultants, and contractors are not covered by this policy but may be required to complete mandatory training at the Company's discretion.

---

## 2. Annual Training Budget

2.1 The Company allocates an individual training budget of **£2,000 per employee per financial year** (1 April to 31 March). This budget covers:

- External training courses and workshops
- Online learning subscriptions (where not centrally provided)
- Examination and certification fees
- Conference attendance
- Books and learning materials

2.2 The individual training budget does **not** cover:

- Mandatory training (see Section 8), which is funded centrally
- Travel and accommodation for training (funded from departmental budgets)
- Professional subscriptions (see Section 5)
- Internal training and mentoring (no cost to the individual budget)

2.3 Unused budget does not carry over to the following financial year.

2.4 Requests to exceed the individual budget may be approved by the employee's Head of Department and the Head of HR in exceptional circumstances (e.g., a high-value qualification directly aligned with business needs). Requests over **£5,000** require approval from the Chief People Officer.

2.5 The total annual Company training budget is reviewed by the Board each financial year as part of the annual business planning cycle. For the financial year 2025/26, the total training budget is **£1,200,000**.

---

## 3. Requesting Training and Development

3.1 All training and development requests should be discussed with the employee's line manager in the first instance, ideally as part of the quarterly Personal Development Plan review (see Section 10).

3.2 Approved requests should be submitted via the Learning Management System (LMS) at least **4 weeks** before the start date of the training.

3.3 The approval process is:

| Budget Required | Approver |
|---|---|
| Up to £500 | Line Manager |
| £501 - £2,000 | Line Manager + Head of Department |
| £2,001 - £5,000 | Head of Department + Head of HR |
| Over £5,000 | Chief People Officer |

3.4 When considering training requests, approvers will take into account:
- Relevance to the employee's current role and career development
- Alignment with business objectives and team needs
- Available budget
- Timing and impact on workload and team capacity
- Whether similar development could be achieved through internal means

3.5 The L&D team will support employees in identifying appropriate training providers and negotiating group discounts where possible.

---

## 4. Conference Attendance

4.1 Employees may attend up to **2 conferences per financial year** with line manager approval. Conference attendance counts towards the individual training budget.

4.2 Conferences that require international travel must also be approved by the Head of Department.

4.3 Where the cost of a conference (including registration, travel, and accommodation) exceeds **£1,500**, a business case should be submitted to the Head of Department justifying attendance.

4.4 Employees who attend conferences are expected to share key takeaways with their team within **2 weeks** of returning, either through a written summary, a team presentation, or a lunch-and-learn session (see Section 12).

4.5 Where the Company sponsors an employee to speak at a conference, registration fees and reasonable travel expenses will be covered from the departmental budget rather than the individual training budget.

## 5. Professional Subscriptions and Memberships

5.1 The Company will pay for **1 relevant professional membership** per employee per year. Examples include:

- British Computer Society (BCS)
- Institution of Engineering and Technology (IET)
- Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development (CIPD)
- Association for Project Management (APM)
- Chartered Management Institute (CMI)
- Institute of Chartered Accountants (ICAEW/ICAS)

5.2 The membership must be relevant to the employee's current role or an agreed development path.

5.3 Requests should be submitted to HR using the Professional Subscription Request Form on the intranet. HR will arrange direct payment or reimburse the employee upon receipt of proof of payment.

5.4 If an employee wishes to hold more than one professional membership, additional memberships may be funded from their individual training budget with line manager approval.

## 6. Study Leave

6.1 Employees pursuing approved professional qualifications or academic courses may take up to **5 paid study leave days per financial year**, in addition to their normal annual leave entitlement.

6.2 Study leave is available for:
- Revision and preparation for examinations directly related to approved qualifications
- Examination days
- Mandatory attendance days for approved academic courses (e.g., university block release)

6.3 Study leave does **not** cover general study time for courses that are not formally approved under this policy.

6.4 To request study leave, employees must:
- Have their course or qualification approved by their line manager and the L&D team
- Submit study leave requests through the HR system at least **2 weeks** in advance
- Provide evidence of examination dates or mandatory attendance requirements

6.5 Additional unpaid study leave may be granted at the discretion of the line manager and Head of Department.

6.6 Study leave for employees in their probation period is not available (see Section 1.3 and Probation Policy HR-PR-011).

## 7. Apprenticeships and Professional Qualifications

### 7.1 Apprenticeship Levy

7.1.1 As an employer with an annual pay bill exceeding £3 million, the Company pays the Apprenticeship Levy. The Company is committed to maximising the use of its Levy funds to develop existing employees and recruit new apprentices.

7.1.2 The Company's annual Apprenticeship Levy contribution is approximately **£75,000** (0.5% of the pay bill minus the £15,000 allowance).

7.1.3 Levy funds may be used for:
- New hire apprenticeships at all levels (Level 2 to Level 7)
- Existing employees undertaking apprenticeship standards (e.g., Digital and Technology Solutions Degree Apprenticeship, Senior Leader Apprenticeship)

7.1.4 Employees interested in pursuing an apprenticeship should contact the L&D team at learning@meridiantech.co.uk.

### 7.2 Professional Qualifications

7.2.1 The Company will support employees to achieve professional qualifications where they are relevant to the business and agreed as part of the employee's Personal Development Plan.

7.2.2 Funding for professional qualifications is drawn from the individual training budget. Where the cost exceeds the annual budget, the Company may agree to fund the balance from the central training budget, subject to the approval process in Section 3.3.

7.2.3 Examples of commonly supported qualifications include:
- AWS Solutions Architect, Developer, or SysOps certifications
- Microsoft Azure certifications
- Google Cloud Professional certifications
- CISSP, CISM (cybersecurity)
- PRINCE2, PMP, Agile certifications
- CIPD qualifications (Level 3, 5, 7)
- ACCA, CIMA (finance)

## 8. Mandatory Training

8.1 All employees are required to complete the following mandatory training. Mandatory training is funded centrally and does **not** count against the individual training budget.

| Training | Frequency | Deadline | Delivery |
|---|---|---|---|
| Health & Safety Induction | Once, on joining | Within **1 week** of start date | In-person / online |
| Data Protection and GDPR | Annually | By **31 March** each year | Online (LMS) |
| Cybersecurity Awareness | Annually | By **31 March** each year | Online (LMS) |
| Equality, Diversity & Inclusion (EDI) | Annually | By **31 March** each year | Online (LMS) |
| Fire Safety Awareness | Annually | By **31 March** each year | Online (LMS) |
| Anti-Bribery and Corruption | Annually | By **31 March** each year | Online (LMS) |
| Modern Slavery Awareness | Once, on joining | Within **1 month** of start date | Online (LMS) |
| Information Security Classification | Once, on joining | Within **1 month** of start date | Online (LMS) |

8.2 New starters must complete all "once on joining" modules within the timescales specified. Annual refresher training must be completed even if the employee joined partway through the year.

8.3 Line managers are responsible for ensuring their team members complete mandatory training. Completion rates are reported monthly by the L&D team.

8.4 Failure to complete mandatory training within the specified timeframe may be addressed through the performance management process. Persistent non-compliance may be treated as a disciplinary matter under the Company's Disciplinary Policy (HR-DP-006).

8.5 For data protection training specifically, see also the Data Protection and Privacy Policy (HR-DP-010), Section 15.

## 9. Clawback Clause

9.1 Where the Company funds external training, courses, or qualifications costing over **£1,000** (inclusive of course fees, examination fees, and materials), the following clawback provisions apply:

9.2 If the employee voluntarily leaves the Company within **12 months** of completing the funded training, they will be required to repay a pro-rata portion of the cost as follows:

| Departure Timing | Repayment |
|---|---|
| Within 3 months of completion | **100%** of the cost |
| 3-6 months after completion | **75%** of the cost |
| 6-9 months after completion | **50%** of the cost |
| 9-12 months after completion | **25%** of the cost |
| After 12 months | No repayment required |

9.3 The clawback applies only where the employee resigns voluntarily. It does not apply if the employee is made redundant, dismissed, or leaves due to ill health.

9.4 Before any training subject to clawback is approved, the employee must sign a Training Agreement acknowledging the clawback terms. The Training Agreement form is available from the L&D team.

9.5 Any repayment due will normally be deducted from the employee's final salary payment, with the employee's written consent. If the final salary is insufficient, the employee will be invoiced for the balance, payable within **30 days**.

9.6 The clawback clause does not apply to mandatory training, conference attendance, or professional subscriptions.

## 10. Personal Development Plans (PDPs)

10.1 Every employee is expected to maintain a Personal Development Plan (PDP), agreed with their line manager.

10.2 PDPs should be:
- **Created** within the first **3 months** of employment (after probation objectives are set; see Probation Policy HR-PR-011)
- **Reviewed quarterly** as part of regular 1-to-1 meetings with the line manager
- **Formally updated** at least **annually**, aligned with the annual performance review cycle (conducted in March each year)

10.3 A PDP should include:
- Short-term development goals (next 3-6 months)
- Medium-term career aspirations (1-2 years)
- Specific training, courses, or experiences to achieve these goals
- Target completion dates
- How development will be measured and evidenced

10.4 The PDP template is available on the Company intranet under HR > Performance > PDP Template.

10.5 PDPs are a collaborative document between the employee and their line manager. Employees are encouraged to take ownership of their development and proactively seek opportunities.

## 11. Internal Mentoring Programme

11.1 The Company operates a formal mentoring programme, coordinated by the L&D team.

11.2 Mentoring is available to all employees who have completed their probation period. Mentees are matched with a mentor from a different department who is at least one level senior.

11.3 Mentoring relationships typically last **6-12 months**, with meetings at least **monthly** (in person or virtual).

11.4 Mentors receive training before taking on a mentoring role. The Company aims to have at least **30 trained mentors** available at any time.

11.5 To apply for a mentor or to volunteer as a mentor, contact the L&D team at learning@meridiantech.co.uk or complete the Mentoring Application Form on the intranet.

11.6 The L&D team will check in with mentor-mentee pairs at the **3-month** and **6-month** marks to ensure the relationship is productive.

## 12. Internal Knowledge Sharing

### 12.1 Lunch and Learn Sessions

12.1.1 The Company runs a regular "Lunch and Learn" programme, held **fortnightly on Wednesdays** from 12:30-13:15. Sessions are open to all employees and are broadcast via Teams for remote attendees.

12.1.2 Topics are employee-led and may include:
- Technical deep-dives
- Project case studies and post-mortems
- New tools and technologies
- Soft skills and leadership
- Industry trends and research

12.1.3 All employees are encouraged to present at least **once per year**. Presenting counts as professional development activity and should be recorded on the PDP.

12.1.4 To propose a session, email learning@meridiantech.co.uk with a title and brief description. The L&D team manages the schedule.

### 12.2 Internal Tech Talks

12.2.1 The Engineering department runs monthly "Tech Talks" on the **last Thursday of each month**, 16:00-17:00. These are more in-depth technical sessions focused on architecture, tooling, and engineering practices.

### 12.3 Learning Library

12.3.1 The Company maintains a digital learning library accessible via the LMS, including:
- LinkedIn Learning (all employees have a licence)
- O'Reilly Media (engineering and product teams)
- Pluralsight (engineering teams)
- Internal course materials and recorded sessions

## 13. Certification Bonuses

13.1 To recognise and incentivise professional certification, the Company offers one-off certification bonuses for employees who pass qualifying examinations.

13.2 Bonus amounts:

| Certification Category | Bonus |
|---|---|
| AWS Certifications (Associate, Professional, Specialty) | **£500** |
| Microsoft Azure Certifications (Associate, Expert, Specialty) | **£500** |
| Google Cloud Professional Certifications | **£500** |
| CISSP, CISM, CISA | **£500** |
| Other industry-recognised certifications (PRINCE2, PMP, Scrum Master, CIPD, etc.) | **£250** |

13.3 To be eligible for a certification bonus:
- The certification must be relevant to the employee's current role or agreed development path
- The employee must have been employed by the Company for at least **6 months** at the time of passing the exam
- The certification must be validated (the employee must provide a copy of the certificate or a verification link)

13.4 Certification bonuses are paid through payroll in the month following validation and are subject to normal tax and National Insurance deductions.

13.5 Certification bonuses are in addition to any reimbursement of examination fees from the individual training budget. An employee may use their training budget for the exam fee **and** receive the bonus on passing.

13.6 Each unique certification qualifies for one bonus only. Renewing an existing certification does not attract a further bonus.

## 14. Monitoring and Review

14.1 The L&D team will monitor and report on the following metrics quarterly:
- Training budget utilisation (per employee, per department, and Company-wide)
- Mandatory training completion rates
- Number of employees with active PDPs
- Mentoring programme participation
- Certification bonuses awarded
- Study leave days taken

14.2 This policy will be reviewed annually by the HR Department and the L&D team.

14.3 Feedback on the Company's learning and development offering is welcomed and can be submitted to learning@meridiantech.co.uk or through the annual employee engagement survey.
